    🍷 Stardew Valley — Wine & Brewing Guide

    Data version: Stardew Valley 1.6.15 | Covers Kegs, Preserves Jars, Dehydrators (1.6), and maximizing artisan profit

    Artisan goods are the backbone of any serious Stardew Valley fortune. A single crop turned into wine can multiply its value by 3× — or more with the Artisan profession. This guide covers every machine, crop comparison, and profit optimization strategy for 1.6.

    🏭 Artisan Goods Overview

    Machine Input Output Base Multiplier Processing Time
    Keg Fruit → Wine Vegetable → Juice 3× 6–7 days (fruit) / 4 days (veg)
    Preserves Jar Fruit → Jam Vegetable → Pickles 2× 2–3 days
    Dehydrator (1.6) Fruit/Mushroom → Dried 1.5× 1 day
    Cheese Press Milk → Cheese 1.2–1.7× 3 hours
    Mayonnaise Machine Egg → Mayo 3× 3 hours
    Loom Wool → Cloth 1.4× 4 hours
    Oil Maker Sunflower → Oil 2× 1 hour
    Smoker (1.6) Fish → Smoked Fish 1.3× 2 hours

    Artisan Profession

    At Farming Level 10, you can choose the Artisan profession. This makes all artisan goods sell for 40% more. That means:

    • Ancient Fruit Wine: 1,650g → 2,310g
    • Starfruit Wine: 3,150g → 4,410g
    • Pale Ale: 300g → 420g

    Tip: Artisan is the single best profession in the game for income. Tiller (Lv 10) gives +10% on raw crops — better at lower levels, but Artisan dominates once you have Kegs.

    🛢️ Keg Mechanics

    How Kegs Work

    Property Value
    Crafting Recipe Farming Lv 8
    Materials 30 Wood + 1 Copper Bar + 1 Iron Bar + 1 Oak Resin
    Input Any fruit (wine) or vegetable (juice)
    Processing Time (Fruit) 6,000 min (≈6.25 days in-game)
    Processing Time (Vegetable) 4,000 min (≈4.17 days)
    Processing Time (Beer/Pale Ale) 1,710 min (≈1.78 days)
    Processing Time (Honey) Not applicable (use Preserves Jar)
    Quality Base quality only (Cask upgrades quality separately)

    Keg Input Categories

    Category Examples Time Base Output Price
    Fruit → Wine Ancient Fruit, Starfruit, Melon 6.25 days 3× fruit value
    Vegetable → Juice Wheat, Beet, Potato 4.17 days 2.25× veg value
    Grain → Beer Wheat (Barley) 1.78 days 200g (always)
    Hops → Pale Ale Hops 1.78 days 300g (always)
    Honey → Mead Honey 2.5 days 200g (always)
    Coffee Bean → Coffee Coffee Bean 0.75 days 150g (always)

    Cask Aging

    Once you have a cellar (house upgrade Lv 3), you can age wine in Casks to improve quality:

    Quality Time Multiplier Sell Price (Ancient Fruit Wine + Artisan)
    Base 0 days 1× 2,310g
    Silver 14 days 1.25× 2,887g
    Gold 28 days 1.5× 3,465g
    Iridium 56 days 2× 4,620g

    Tip: Only age Ancient Fruit Wine and Starfruit Wine in the cellar. Pale Ale and lower-value wines aren't worth the cask space (the profit-per-day drops significantly). Fill your cellar once with Starfruit Wine, let it hit Iridium quality over 56 days, and collect 4,620g per bottle.

    The Two Kings: Ancient Fruit vs Starfruit

    Aspect Ancient Fruit Starfruit
    Wine Price (Artisan) 2,310g 4,410g
    Growth Time 28 days (then regrows 7 days) 13 days (no regrowth)
    Cost per Harvest Free (regrows) 400g (replant)
    Seed Source Artifact → Seed Maker Oasis shop (400g)
    Kegs per Season ~4 harvests (Greenhouse/Island) ~2 harvests (Greenhouse/Island)
    Year-round? Yes (Greenhouse/Island) Yes (Greenhouse/Island)
    Cask Value Good (2,310→4,620) Best (4,410→8,820)

    Verdict: Ancient Fruit wins for hands-off profit. Plant once in the Greenhouse, harvest every 7 days forever, never replant. Starfruit wins for maximum profit per keg but requires buying seeds and replanting. Use both: Ancient Fruit for bulk, Starfruit for cask aging.

    Tip: A full Greenhouse of Ancient Fruit (120 tiles) with Artisan profession produces 120 Ancient Fruit Wine every 7 days = 277,200g/week. That's over 1 million gold per season with zero replanting.

    🫙 Preserves Jar vs Keg Comparison

    Aspect Preserves Jar Keg
    Multiplier 2× (fruit = jam, veg = pickles) 3× (fruit = wine, veg = juice)
    Processing Time 2–3 days 6.25 days (wine)
    Best For High-yield / low-value crops High-value / single-harvest crops
    Pumpkin ~~2520g~~ actually 965g (2.62×) 720g as juice (2.25×)
    Blueberry (3 berries) 630g (4.2× per tile) 420g (2.8× per tile)
    Cranberries (2 berries) 525g (3.5× per tile) 315g (2.1× per tile)
    Eggplant 525g as pickles 315g as juice

    When to Use Preserves Jar Instead of Keg

    Preserves Jar wins when:

    1. Multiple-harvest crops — Blueberries (3 per bush) and Cranberries (2 per bush) get higher total value per day from the Jar because it processes faster and the multiplier applies per berry
    2. Short seasons — In Spring, the Preserves Jar can process 3 batches in the time a Keg does 1, making it better for rapid turnover
    3. Filling gaps — You have more crops than Kegs. Jar processes 2–3× faster, so it catches overflow

    Keg wins when:

    1. High-value single crops — Starfruit, Ancient Fruit, Melon, Pumpkin
    2. You have Artisan profession — The +40% applies equally, but 3× base beats 2× base
    3. You have Casks — Only wine can be aged in the cellar

    Quick Decision Table

    Crop Type Better Machine Why
    Ancient Fruit Keg 3× multiplier, cask aging
    Starfruit Keg 3× multiplier, cask aging
    Pumpkin Preserves Jar 2.62× vs 2.25× juice
    Melon Keg 3× wine beats 2× jam
    Blueberry Preserves Jar 4.2× per tile vs 2.8×
    Cranberry Preserves Jar 3.5× per tile vs 2.1×
    Strawberry Keg Regrows + 3× wine
    Hot Pepper Keg Regrows daily + 3× wine
    Corn Preserves Jar Seasonal + 2.62× pickles
    Hops Keg only Pale Ale (300g)
    Wheat Keg only Beer (200g)

    🍺 Pale Ale vs Wine

    Pale Ale deserves special attention because of its fast processing time.

    Aspect Pale Ale Ancient Fruit Wine Starfruit Wine
    Processing Time 1.78 days 6.25 days 6.25 days
    Base Price 300g 1,650g 3,150g
    Artisan Price 420g 2,310g 4,410g
    Gold per Keg/Day 236g 370g 706g
    Regrowth Daily Every 7 days No regrowth
    Seeds 50g (Pierre) Free (Seed Maker) 400g (Oasis)

    Pale Ale is the best early-to-mid game artisan good. Hops grow on a trellis, produce daily after 11 days, and Pale Ale takes only 1.78 days to process. With just 30 Hops plants and 15 Kegs, you can make 6,300g/day (Artisan). That's over 175,000g per season from a 30-tile trellis section.

    When to switch: Once you have Ancient Fruit set up in the Greenhouse and Ginger Island, Ancient Fruit Wine becomes more profitable per Keg and less daily work. Pale Ale is better for Year 1–2; Ancient Fruit Wine dominates Year 3+.

    🗺️ Optimal Keg Layout Strategy

    Keg Count Targets

    Phase Kegs Crop Support Daily Income (Artisan)
    Early (Y1 Summer) 5–10 20 Blueberries + 10 Hops 5,000g/day
    Mid (Y1 Fall) 20–30 30 Hops + Greenhouse Ancient Fruit 15,000g/day
    Late (Y2 Spring) 60–80 Full Greenhouse (120 tiles) + Island start 40,000g/day
    Endgame (Y2+) 200+ Island + Greenhouse + Starfruit rotation 100,000g+/day

    Best Layout Pattern

    A Big Shed fits 67 Kegs (optimal layout):

    Floor: Wood floor or crystal path
Rows: 6 columns of kegs, 1-wide paths between
Pattern: K K K K K K (path) K K K K K K
         67 Kegs per Big Shed

    For Ginger Island:

    Build 2 Big Sheds = 134 Kegs
One shed for Ancient Fruit Wine
One shed for Starfruit Wine (or overflow)
Place near island farm for easy access

    Keg Material Requirements

    Keg Count Wood Copper Bar Iron Bar Oak Resin
    10 300 10 10 10
    50 1,500 50 50 50
    100 3,000 100 100 100
    200 6,000 200 200 200

    Oak Resin is the bottleneck. Start tapping every Oak tree on your farm in Year 1 Spring. Plant an Oak forest (20+ trees) on the train tracks or desert for infinite Resin.

    Tip: In 1.6, you can craft Tree Fertilizer (from Foraging Mastery) to speed up Oak tree growth. Apply it to planted acorns and you'll have tappable trees in 5 days instead of 20+.

    🏆 Artisan Profession Details

    Profession Path Lv 5 Lv 10 Best For
    Farming → Artisan Tiller (+10% crop value) Artisan (+40% artisan goods) Everything (best)
    Farming → Agriculturist Tiller (+10% crop value) Agriculturist (+10% growth speed) Min-max speedruns

    Artisan is the undisputed best Lv 10 Farming profession for profit. The +40% applies to:

    • Wine, Juice, Pale Ale, Beer, Mead, Coffee, Green Tea
    • Cheese, Goat Cheese
    • Mayonnaise, Duck Mayonnaise, Dinosaur Mayonnaise
    • Cloth
    • Honey
    • Jelly, Pickles, Dried Fruit (1.6 dehydrator)
    • Smoked Fish (1.6 smoker)
    • Truffle Oil

    1.6 Dehydrator Bonus

    The 1.6 Dehydrator turns fruit into dried fruit that sells for 1.5× base. With Artisan:

    Fruit Base Dried Artisan Dried
    Ancient Fruit 550g 825g 1,155g
    Starfruit 750g 1,125g 1,575g
    Melon 250g 375g 525g

    Dried fruit has one major advantage: it doesn't spoil or require cask aging. However, it's still less profitable per fruit than wine, and the 1-day processing time means it competes with Preserves Jar for your fruit supply.

    Tip: The Dehydrator is niche. Use it for excess fruit when all your Kegs are full, or for low-value fruit (blueberries, cranberries) where the fast processing time benefits you. Never put Starfruit or Ancient Fruit in a Dehydrator — wine is always better.

    💡 Artisan Tips & Tricks

    1. Always choose Artisan profession — The +40% bonus is game-changing. Only Agriculturist wins in speedrun scenarios.
    2. Oak Resin is king — Start tapping Oak trees on Day 1 of Spring Year 1. Plant an oak forest on your farm or train tracks.
    3. Greenhouse Ancient Fruit first — Fill the Greenhouse with Ancient Fruit before anything else. One seed becomes infinite through Seed Makers.
    4. Pale Ale for early cash — Hops produce daily and Pale Ale processes in under 2 days. It's the best early-game money maker.
    5. Casks are for Starfruit only — With 189 cask slots in the cellar, fill once with Starfruit Wine, wait 56 days, collect 4,410g × 189 = 833,490g.
    6. Preserves Jar for multiple-harvest crops — Blueberries and Cranberries get better per-tile value in Jars than Kegs.
    7. Ginger Island is your second Keg shed — Build 2 Big Sheds on the island farm, fill with Kegs. Process fruit on-site.
    8. 1.6 Dehydrator for overflow — When your Kegs are full and you have excess fruit, Dehydrators process in 1 day and free up space.
    9. Smoker (+1.3× fish) is new in 1.6 — Smoked fish sells well, uses the same Keg shed area, and doesn't compete with fruit processing.
