    🍑 Complete Fruit Tree Guide

    Data version: Stardew Valley 1.6.15 | All fruit trees, profit analysis, payback period, and orchard layout optimization

    Fruit trees are a long-term investment in Stardew Valley. Unlike seasonal crops that require replanting, a single fruit tree produces fruit every day of its season (and year-round in the Greenhouse or on Ginger Island) for the rest of the game. This guide breaks down exactly which trees pay off fastest and how to maximize them.

    Data sourced from Stardew Valley Wiki v1.6

    🌳 Fruit Tree Basics

    Rule Detail
    Purchase Pierre's General Store at the start of each season
    Growth 28 days to mature — plant a full season before harvest
    Harvest 1 fruit per day during the tree's season
    Quality Increases by 1 star per year of maturity (Base → Silver → Gold → Iridium after 4 years)
    Tile Needs the 8 surrounding tiles completely clear (no path, no grass, no sprinkler)
    Winter Fruit trees do NOT produce in Winter (except Greenhouse/Ginger Island)
    Lightning Can be struck by lightning in Summer — use Lightning Rods nearby

    Greenhouse & Ginger Island

    In the Greenhouse (unlocked via Pantry bundles) or on Ginger Island, fruit trees produce year-round. This makes them dramatically more profitable — instead of 28 days of harvest per year, they produce 112 days (every season).

    💰 Fruit Tree Profit Table (Base Quality)

    Tree Season Sapling Cost Fruit Price Days Producing Total Gross/Year Daily Profit
    🍑 Peach Summer 6,000g 140g 28 3,920g -74.29g/day
    🍎 Pomegranate Fall 6,000g 140g 28 3,920g -74.29g/day
    🍊 Orange Summer 4,000g 100g 28 2,800g -42.86g/day
    🍒 Cherry Spring 3,400g 80g 28 2,240g -41.43g/day
    🍑 Apricot Spring 2,000g 50g 28 1,400g -21.43g/day
    🍐 Apple Fall 4,000g 100g 28 2,800g -42.86g/day

    "Daily Profit" is negative because fruit trees require a full year to pay back their sapling cost. They're an investment, not short-term profit. The real value comes from Keg processing + long-term Iridium quality scaling.

    Year-by-Year Profit (Outdoors, Single Tree)

    Tree Year 1 Year 2 (Silver) Year 3 (Gold) Year 4+ (Iridium)
    Peach -2,080g +2,080g +4,184g +7,000g
    Pomegranate -2,080g +2,080g +4,184g +7,000g
    Orange -1,200g +1,200g +2,600g +4,480g
    Cherry -1,160g +1,160g +2,360g +3,920g
    Apricot -600g +600g +1,400g +2,520g
    Apple -1,200g +1,200g +2,600g +4,480g

    Key Insight: A Peach tree doesn't break even until Year 3 outdoors. But in the Greenhouse, it breaks even in Year 1 (112 days × 140g = 15,680g gross, net +9,680g).

    Fruit tree fruits can be processed in Kegs or Preserves Jars for significantly more value.

    Keg vs. Jar for Fruit Trees

    Fruit Keg Wine (Artisan) Jar Jelly (Artisan) Best Choice
    Peach 588g (7 days) 441g (2.5 days) Jar — faster throughput, only 25% less per item
    Pomegranate 588g (7 days) 441g (2.5 days) Jar
    Orange 420g (7 days) 329g (2.5 days) Jar
    Cherry 336g (7 days) 273g (2.5 days) Jar
    Apple 420g (7 days) 329g (2.5 days) Jar
    Apricot 210g (7 days) 189g (2.5 days) Jar

    Tip: Unlike Starfruit and ancient crops, fruit tree fruits should go in Preserves Jars, not Kegs. The production speed of Jars (2.5 days vs 7 days for Kegs) means you process 2.8× more fruit per machine per season. With Artisan, Jar jelly is only ~25% less per item, but you process nearly 3× the volume.

    🧮 Payback Period Analysis

    Tree Sapling Outdoors Payback (Raw) Outdoors (Wine) Greenhouse (Wine)
    Apricot 🍑 2,000g Year 3 (1.4yr base) Year 1 ~3 months
    Cherry 🍒 3,400g Year 4 (1.5yr base) Year 2 ~5 months
    Orange 🍊 4,000g Year 4 (1.4yr base) Year 2 ~5 months
    Peach 🍑 6,000g Year 4 (1.5yr base) Year 3 ~6 months
    Apple 🍏 4,000g Year 4 (1.4yr base) Year 2 ~5 months
    Pomegranate 🍎 6,000g Year 4 (1.5yr base) Year 3 ~6 months

    Apricot is the fastest-paying fruit tree — lowest sapling cost (2,000g), payback in ~1.4 years outdoors with raw sales, or 3 months in the Greenhouse.

    🏠 Greenhouse Orchard Layout

    Optimal 16-Tree Layout

    The greenhouse interior has a tillable soil area plus a border of non-tillable tiles around the edge. Fruit trees can be planted on the non-tillable border tiles (stone/wood flooring) — this is the standard greenhouse orchard setup.

      +---------------------------------------+
  |  🍑  🍒  🍊  🍑  🍎  🍒  🍊  🍎   | ← Trees on border
  |  🍑  🍒  🍊  🍑  🍎  🍒  🍊  🍎   | ← Trees on border
  |  🌾  🌾  🌾  🌾  🌾  🌾  🌾  🌾    | ← Crops in center
  |  🌾  🌾  🌾  🌾  🌾  🌾  🌾  🌾    |
  |  🌾  🌾  🌾  🌾  🌾  🌾  🌾  🌾    |
  |  🍑  🍒  🍊  🍑  🍎  🍒  🍊  🍎   | ← Trees on border
  |  🍑  🍒  🍊  🍑  🍎  🍒  🍊  🍎   | ← Trees on border
  +---------------------------------------+

    16 trees = 2 of each fruit type = 16 fruit/day = 11,200g/day (base price, with Artisan Jelly → ~4,400g/day processing profit).

    Tip: The greenhouse border fits exactly 18 trees (9 on each long side). Plant 2 of each fruit type plus 6 extras of the highest-value fruits (Peach, Pomegranate). Leave the center 10×12 tiles for Starfruit or Ancient Fruit.

    📅 Seasonal Fruit Tree Planting Calendar

    Plant trees 28 days before their season starts so they mature exactly in time.

    Tree Buy Seeds Plant By Harvest Season
    Apricot 🍑 Spring Y1 Spring 1 Spring
    Cherry 🍒 Spring Y1 Spring 1 Spring
    Orange 🍊 Summer Y1 Summer 1 Summer
    Peach 🍑 Summer Y1 Summer 1 Summer
    Apple 🍏 Fall Y1 Fall 1 Fall
    Pomegranate 🍎 Fall Y1 Fall 1 Fall

    Year 1 Priority

    1. Greenhouse trees: Plant 1 of each type as soon as you unlock the Greenhouse (mid-to-late Y1)
    2. Outdoor trees: Only if you have spare cash after upgrading tools/barn/coop
    3. Apricot and Cherry go in Spring — early, cheap
    4. Peach and Pomegranate are expensive — save these for Greenhouse

    Tip: Don't plant fruit trees outdoors in Year 1 unless you have surplus cash. The Greenhouse is where fruit trees earn their keep. Focus on getting the Greenhouse repaired first, then plant trees inside.

    🏆 Best Fruit Trees Ranked

    Rank Tree Why
    🥇 Peach 🍑 Highest fruit sell price (140g), loved by Robin, great in Greenhouse
    🥇 Pomegranate 🍎 Same 140g fruit, loved by Elliott, also top-tier
    🥈 Orange 🍊 100g fruit, loved by Gus, solid mid-tier
    🥈 Apple 🍏 100g fruit, needed for Fodder bundle (3 Apples), loved by Kent
    🥉 Cherry 🍒 80g fruit, loved by some villagers, cheap sapling
    🥉 Apricot 🍑 50g fruit but cheapest sapling (2,000g), fastest payback

    Gifting Value

    Fruit tree fruits make excellent universal liked gifts (most NPCs like or love at least one):

    NPC Loved Fruit Tree Gift
    Robin Peach 🍑
    Elliott Pomegranate 🍎
    Gus Orange 🍊
    Kent Apple (surprisingly — fried food)
    Evelyn Tulip (crop, not tree) — but all fruits are liked

    Tip: Keep a chest near your fruit trees with one of each fruit type for gifting. A single Peach given to Robin on her birthday (Fall 21) gives 640 friendship points — worth more than the 140g sale price.

    ⚡ Ginger Island Fruit Tree Farm

    On Ginger Island, fruit trees produce year-round with no winter downtime. This is the optimal location for a mass orchard:

    Setup Trees Daily Fruit Daily Profit (Raw) Daily Profit (Jelly)
    Small 4 (2 Peach + 2 Pomegranate) 4 560g 1,764g (Jelly, Artisan)
    Medium 12 (2 of each) 12 1,440g 3,528g (Jelly, Artisan)
    Large 24 (4 of each) 24 2,880g 7,056g (Jelly, Artisan)
    Massive 48 (8 of each) 48 5,760g 14,112g (Jelly, Artisan)

    Ginger Island orchard strategy: Plant 2-4 of each fruit tree on the island farm's non-tillable soil. With Iridium quality after 4 years, each fruit sells for 280g raw — a 48-tree orchard generates 13,440g/day completely passively.
