    🤿 Coral Island — Diving & Ocean Restoration Guide

    Diving is Coral Island's signature mechanic — it replaces the traditional mining gameplay loop with underwater ocean cleaning. Unlike Stardew's mines or Mistria's caverns, diving here is about restoration: collecting trash, salvaging artifacts, and progressively unlocking deeper zones as you clean the ocean floor.

    🔓 Unlock Conditions

    Requirement Details
    Day Day 3 (after the introductory quests)
    Quest "The Ocean Needs You" — delivered via mail after Day 2
    Tool Diving gear is provided by Ling (dock, south of farm)
    Cost Free — no upfront investment
    Prerequisite Must clear 5 trash from the shoreline (quest step)

    Tip: Don't rush to Mining on Day 3. Grab your diving gear from Ling and spend Days 3–5 exclusively underwater. The early trash haul funds your seed purchases for Spring faster than any other activity.

    🌊 Ocean Floor Structure

    The ocean is divided into 5 distinct zones, unlocked by raising your Ocean Rank via cleaning trash.

    Zone Ocean Rank Needed Depth Trash Density Notable Loot
    🌿 Shoreline (Zone 1) None 0–5m Medium Glass, Scrap, Common Artifacts
    🪸 Coral Garden (Zone 2) Bronze B 5–15m High Kelp, Glass, Shells, Scrolls
    ⚓ Shipwreck Field (Zone 3) Silver B 15–25m Very High Metal Scrap, Machinery Parts, Gold Chests
    🏛️ Ancient Ruins (Zone 4) Gold A 25–40m Medium Precious Relics, Crystals, Rare Artifacts
    🌌 Deep Trench (Zone 5) Osmium S 40–60m Sparse Osmium Nodes, Legendary Artifacts

    Fast Travel Points

    Unlocked at Ocean Rank Gold A. Costs 200g to activate each beacon:

    Beacon Zone Unlock Cost
    Dock Beacon Shoreline Free
    Coral Arch Coral Garden 200g
    Wreck Bridge Shipwreck Field 400g
    Temple Gate Ancient Ruins 750g
    Abyss Entrance Deep Trench 1200g

    🗑️ Trash-to-Treasure Conversion

    Every piece of ocean trash can be processed through recycling machines. This is Coral Island's primary material loop.

    Complete Recycling Table

    Trash Type Base Sell Recycled Output Output Sell Value Net Gain
    🥤 Plastic Bottle 3g 1 Plastic Scrap 5g +2g
    🥫 Tin Can 4g 1 Copper Scrap 8g +4g
    🪵 Driftwood 5g 2 Wood 4g −1g
    🔩 Rusty Gear 8g 2 Iron Scrap 24g +16g
    🧵 Old Cloth 3g 2 Fabric 10g +7g
    🪞 Broken Glass 2g 2 Glass 6g +4g
    🔗 Rope 2g 1 Fiber 3g +1g
    ⚙️ Machine Part 15g 1 Gold Scrap 20g +5g
    ⌛ Ceramic Pot 6g 2 Clay 8g +2g
    🔋 Battery 12g 1 Scrap Battery 25g +13g
    📦 Cardboard 1g 1 Paper 2g +1g

    Scrap → Refined Materials

    Scrap Type Smelts Into Craft Value Use
    Copper Scrap ×3 1 Copper Bar 60g Tools, Sprinklers
    Iron Scrap ×3 1 Iron Bar 150g Upgraded tools, Machines
    Gold Scrap ×3 1 Gold Bar 400g Late tools, Jewelry
    Osmium Scrap ×3 1 Osmium Bar 1200g Endgame tools, Auto-collectors
    Glass ×5 2 Refined Glass 40g Windows, Decorations

    Tip: Never sell Glass directly. Store it. Refined Glass is required for the Sprinkler II upgrade and for several museum donation rewards. You'll need 60+ Refined Glass for mid-game automation alone.

    🧹 Cleanup Progression Strategy

    First Pass (Days 3–15) — Profit Focus

    Goal: Clear visible trash in Zone 1 and Zone 2. Sell most recyclable output immediately.

    Priority Item Why
    1 Rusty Gear Highest net gain per inventory slot (+16g)
    2 Battery +13g per item
    3 Machine Part +5g per item, but rare
    4 Ceramic Pot +2g per item, good volume
    Everything else — Sell or recycle — don't hoard yet

    Expected income: 1500–2500g per diving session (2 in-game hours)

    Second Pass (Summer+ ) — Materials Focus

    Goal: Clean remaining trash, but keep all materials for upgrades.

    Keep? Material Reason
    ✅ Keep Iron Scrap, Gold Scrap Tool upgrades (watering can → iron, axe → gold)
    ✅ Keep Glass, Refined Glass Sprinkler II × Sprinkler III path
    ✅ Keep Osmium Scrap Rare — every piece counts for Osmium tools
    ❌ Sell Plastic, Rope, Cardboard Low value, no important crafts
    ❌ Sell Driftwood Actually a net loss to recycle (−1g) — sell raw instead

    👾 Underwater Enemies

    Not all underwater creatures are friendly. These will attack you:

    Enemy Zone HP Attack Drop Strategy
    🦐 Aggressive Shrimp Zone 1–2 20 Bump damage (8 HP) Shrimp Meat (sell 15g) Just swim away — slower than you
    🐡 Pufferfish Zone 2–3 40 Poison spit (15 HP + DoT) Toxic Spine (alchemy) Circle-strafe — spit travels in a straight line
    🦑 Ink Squid Zone 3–4 60 Ink cloud (blinds 3s, 12 HP) Ink Sac (sell 25g) Swim upward when cloud appears — cloud sinks
    🦈 Reef Shark Zone 4–5 150 Bite charge (40 HP), Tail slap (25 HP) Shark Tooth (sell 80g) Never attack head-on. Hug walls, let it charge past, pick up loot behind it
    👻 Phantom Jelly Zone 5 only 80 Shock tether (20 HP + stun 2s) Void Gel (rare crafting) Avoid entirely. Drops nothing worth fighting for

    Tip: You don't need to kill anything underwater. Unlike mines, enemies don't block your path — the ocean is open terrain. The only reason to fight is for a specific drop. Sharks drop Shark Tooth (80g) which makes them worth farming in Zone 4 for money.

    🏛️ Artifact Collecting

    Underwater artifacts are essential for both the museum and cash.

    Artifact Zone Rarity Museum Value Sell Price Notes
    Coral Fragment 1–2 Common 400 rep 50g Extremely common
    Ancient Coin 2–3 Uncommon 800 rep 200g Also donation quota filler
    Sea Scroll 3 Uncommon 1000 rep 350g Multiple scrolls needed
    Gold Idol 3–4 Rare 1500 rep 800g Only 3 in the entire ocean
    Crystal Skull 4 Rare 2000 rep 1200g Museum centerpiece
    Star Shard 5 Legendary 3500 rep 2500g Drops from Phantom Jelly (1%)
    Pearl Necklace 4 Rare 1800 rep 1000g Inside gold chests only
    Ancient Tablet 5 Legendary 3000 rep 1800g Unlocks secret quest

    Museum Donation Priority: 1. Donate 1 of each until the set is complete (bonus reward per set) 2. Sell all duplicates immediately — they're worth more vendor gold than museum black pearls 3. Exception: Star Shard — donate even duplicates; each donation grants +3500 rep (rep accelerates Ocean Rank)

    💨 Stamina & Oxygen Management

    Diving uses a combined stamina/oxygen resource. Everything costs oxygen.

    Action Oxygen Cost Notes
    Swimming (empty) 1 / sec Base speed
    Sprint swimming 3 / sec 2× speed, 3× oxygen burn
    Collect trash 5 per item
    Open chest 10 per chest
    Fight (dodge) 15 per dodge
    Passive regen −1 / 3 sec Only when standing still

    Oxygen Tank Upgrades

    Tank Level Max Oxygen Cost Unlock
    Basic (T1) 200 Free Quest reward
    Reinforced (T2) 350 500g + 10 Iron Bar Ocean Rank Copper B
    Deluxe (T3) 550 2000g + 8 Gold Bar Ocean Rank Silver A
    Premium (T4) 800 5000g + 5 Osmium Bar Ocean Rank Gold A
    Ultimate (T5) 1200 8000g + 8 Osmium Bar Ocean Rank Osmium S

    Best Food for Diving

    Food Oxygen Restore Effect Season Get
    Seaweed Salad +50 O₂ None All Craft (Kelp ×2) — costs nothing
    Grilled Fish +100 O₂ +3 DEF 5 min All Cook (Any Fish)
    Coconut Water +80 O₂ +1 SPD 3 min Summer Forage from palm trees
    Clam Chowder +200 O₂ +5 DEF 8 min Spring/Fall Cook (Clam + Milk + Potato)
    Lobster Bisque +350 O₂ +10 DEF 10 min Fall/Winter Cook (Lobster + Cream + Butter)

    Tip: Seaweed Salad is the most oxygen-efficient food in the game. Each Kelp costs 0g (forage from Shoreline) and restores 50 O₂. Carry 10 Seaweed Salads on every dive — they're free, stack, and keep you underwater for an extra 500 O₂ per inventory slot.

    💰 Profit Analysis: Diving vs. Fishing vs. Mining

    Activity Early Game (Spring) Mid Game (Summer) Late Game (Fall+) Best Season
    🤿 Diving 1500–2500g/day 2000–3500g/day 3000–5000g/day Spring (no crop income yet)
    🎣 Fishing 800–1200g/day 1200–2000g/day 2500–4000g/day Fall (salmon run)
    ⛏️ Mining 500–1000g/day 3500–6000g/day 5000–8000g/day Summer (gold ore nodes)

    Seasonal Diving Profit Notes

    Season Adjustment
    🌸 Spring Diving is your #1 income source until crops mature. Day 3–8 diving funds all seed purchases
    ☀️ Summer Mining overtakes diving. Shift focus — mine for gold, dive only for artifacts and upgrades
    🍂 Fall Diving picks up again (Coral Garden has a seasonal artifact spawn event). Shipwreck Field ×2 drops
    ❄️ Winter Diving is viable year-round. Winter fishing drops but diving stays steady. Use to fund Osmium upgrades

    🚀 Deep Dive Tips

    1. Inventory management is critical. Each dive yields 20–40 items. If your inventory fills, the best items (gold chests, Star Shards) decay after 30 seconds. Prioritize: Legendary artifacts > Gold chests > Gems > Metal scraps > Glass > Wood.
    2. Zone 2 is your farm spot. Coral Garden has the highest trash density per square meter. Run Zone 2 horizontally, collect everything, surface, recycle, repeat.
    3. Ocean Rank progression path: Bronze B (need 20 trash) → Silver B (need 100 trash) → Gold A (need 300 trash). Each rank doubles the area you can explore. Spend your early game rush on raw trash collection, not artifact hunting.
    4. Upgrade tank before fin speed. More oxygen lets you stay down longer = more trash per session. Fin speed doesn't help if you run out of oxygen.
    5. Gold chests spawn on a timer. Every 5 in-game hours in a fully-cleaned zone, a gold chest respawns. Farm Gold Chest routes once you reach Gold A rank.
    6. Rainy days double trash spawns. The storm stirs up the ocean floor — new trash appears at 2× rate. Dive on every rainy day without exception.
