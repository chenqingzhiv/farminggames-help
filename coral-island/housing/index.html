    🏠 Coral Island Housing Guide — Home Upgrades, Decorations, and Room-by-Room Tips

    Your house in Coral Island isn't just a place to sleep. A well-decorated home gives you stamina bonuses, cooking perks, and even boosts your relationship with visitors. This guide walks you through every upgrade tier, the best furniture to craft, and how to max out your Comfort Score.

    Tip: Unlike Stardew Valley, Coral Island's housing system has a Comfort Score that directly affects your daily energy regeneration. More comfort = more energy every morning.

    🏗️ House Upgrades — Tiers and Costs

    The Carpenter (on the northern edge of town) handles all house upgrades. Each tier expands your floor space and unlocks new features.

    Upgrade Cost Materials Required Rooms Unlocked Comfort Boost
    🏚️ Starter Tent Free — 1 (bedroom) 0
    🪵 Wood Cabin 2,500g Wood ×50, Stone ×30 2 +5
    🪨 Stone House 5,000g Hardwood ×50, Silver Bar ×10, Stone ×80 3 +10
    🧱 Brick House 10,000g Gold Bar ×15, Hardwood ×100, Brick ×50 4 +15
    🏛️ Manor 25,000g Osmium Bar ×20, Refined Quartz ×20, Titanium Ore ×50 5 +25
    🏰 Mansion 50,000g Osmium Bar ×40, Titanium Bar ×20, Enriched Ore ×100 6+ +40
    Room Unlocked At Base Size
    🛌 Bedroom Starter 5×5
    🍳 Kitchen Wood Cabin 5×4
    🪴 Sunroom Stone House 4×4
    🧪 Craft Room Brick House 5×5
    🛁 Bathroom Manor 4×3
    🎮 Entertainment Room Mansion 6×5

    Tip: Skip the Wood Cabin if you can. Save up for the Stone House directly — it costs more upfront but the extra room and higher Comfort boost are worth the wait.

    🛋️ Furniture Crafting & Acquisition

    You get furniture from three main sources: crafting at the workbench, buying from the furniture catalog, and finding rare pieces while diving or mining.

    Craftable Furniture

    Item Materials Comfort Value Best For
    🛏️ Basic Bed Wood ×20, Fiber ×10 +2 Starting bedroom
    🛏️ Comfort Bed Hardwood ×15, Cloth ×5, Silver Bar ×2 +5 Bedroom (mid-game)
    🛏️ Luxury Bed Gold Bar ×5, Silk ×3, Osmium Bar ×2 +10 Bedroom (end-game)
    🪑 Wood Chair Wood ×8 +1 Kitchen/dining
    🛋️ Comfy Sofa Hardwood ×10, Cloth ×8 +6 Living room
    🗄️ Dresser Wood ×15, Silver Bar ×2 +3 Bedroom storage
    📚 Bookshelf Hardwood ×12, Paper ×5 +4 Craft Room
    🪴 Potted Plant Wood ×5, Fiber ×15, Clay ×3 +2 Any room
    🖼️ Wall Painting Hardwood ×5, Cloth ×10, Dye ×2 +5 Living room/walls
    💡 Standing Lamp Gold Bar ×2, Glass ×5 +3 Lighting (any room)
    🧶 Rug (Small) Cloth ×10, Fiber ×20 +3 Bedroom/bathroom
    🧶 Rug (Large) Cloth ×25, Fiber ×40 +6 Living room

    Furniture Catalog (Buyable)

    Once you upgrade to at least Stone House, the Furniture Catalog becomes available at the general store. These items cost more gold but require no materials:

    Item Price Comfort Notes
    Tropical Wallpaper Set 2,000g +4 per wall Covers entire room
    Ocean View Window 1,500g +3 Kitchen only
    Seashell Chandelier 3,000g +7 Living room centerpiece
    Bamboo Flooring 1,800g +3 Any room
    Aquarium (Small) 2,500g +5 Place fish inside!

    Tip: The Aquarium is the best comfort-per-slot item in the game. Fill it with rare fish and it gives bonus comfort based on fish quality.

    🛏️ Decoration Tips — Room by Room

    🛌 Bedroom

    This is your most important room. The bed determines your base stamina recovery — upgrading from Basic to Luxury Bed bumps you from +2 to +10 Comfort.

    Setup Checklist: - Luxury Bed (priority #1) - Dresser (storage + comfort) - Large Rug (biggest single comfort item for floor space) - Bookshelf in any corner - Potted plant by the window

    🍳 Kitchen

    The kitchen unlocks cooking, which is how you get the best energy-restoring meals and gift items.

    Setup Checklist: - Stove & Fridge (base cooking) - Counter surfaces (decorative, adds +2 comfort) - Small table with potted plant - Wall shelves for spice jars (craftable, +1 comfort each)

    🪴 Sunroom

    This room gives passive bonuses to crops. Decorating it with natural items (potted plants, outdoor-themed furniture) boosts the effect.

    Setup Checklist: - 4+ Potted Plants - Natural Rug (grass/leaf pattern) - Window decorations - Water feature (fountain decor, +5 comfort)

    🧪 Craft Room

    Reduces the material cost of crafting by 5-15% depending on Comfort Score.

    Setup Checklist: - Workbench (center) - Bookshelves along walls - Tool Rack (craftable, +3 comfort) - Floor lamps for good lighting

    🛁 Bathroom

    The bathroom restores a small amount of stamina when you use it each morning — about 5-10 energy depending on Comfort Score.

    Setup Checklist: - Bathtub (craftable or catalog) - Small Rug - Wall mirror (+3 comfort) - Potted fern

    🎮 Entertainment Room

    End-game room that gives a daily Luck buff. Friends also hang out here when they visit.

    Setup Checklist: - Sofa and coffee table - Arcade machine (catalog, 5,000g) - Wall decoration set - Large rug

    📊 Comfort Score System — How It Works

    Your Comfort Score is the sum of every furniture piece's Comfort value in your house. The score determines your daily stamina recovery and some passive bonuses.

    Comfort Score Stamina Recovered Bonus Effects
    0-10 15 None
    11-25 25 None
    26-50 35 Crafting cost -5%
    51-80 50 Crafting cost -10%, Cooking buff +25%
    81-120 70 Crafting cost -15%, Cooking buff +50%, Luck +1
    120+ 100 All bonuses, +2 Luck, morning buff "Well Rested"

    The Well Rested buff (at 120+ Comfort) gives you +10% movement speed for the first 4 hours of the day. That's an extra 20-30 actions before noon.

    How to Reach 120+ Comfort

    Strategy Approx Cost Comfort Gained Notes
    Max house upgrade 50,000g +40 base Required to unlock all rooms
    Luxury Bed Hardwood×20, Gold×5, Osmium×2 +10 Single best furniture piece
    Large Rug ×4 Cloth×100, Fiber×160 +24 Fill every floor
    Aquarium (filled) 2,500g + rare fish +8-12 Depends on fish quality
    Wall Decorations ×6 Mixed materials +18 Use every wall slot
    Potted Plants ×5 Wood×25, Fiber×75, Clay×15 +10 Cheap comfort
    Chandelier 3,000g (catalog) +7 Living room ceiling

    Tip: Cloth becomes the bottleneck for high Comfort scores. Set up a loom early and keep it running. You'll need dozens of cloth pieces for rugs and sofas.

    📦 Storage Solutions

    You'll accumulate a lot of stuff. Here's how to keep organized:

    Storage Type Capacity Materials Best For
    📦 Wood Box 36 slots Wood ×20 Early game, general storage
    📦 Iron Box 48 slots Iron Bar ×5, Wood ×15 Mid-game
    📦 Gold Box 60 slots Gold Bar ×5, Hardwood ×10 Late-game
    📦 Osmium Box 72 slots Osmium Bar ×5, Titanium Bar ×3 End-game
    🧊 Fridge 36 slots (food) Gold Bar ×3, Glass ×5 Cooking ingredients
    🗄️ Dresser 24 slots (clothes) Hardwood×10, Cloth×5 Clothing and accessories

    Organization Strategy: - Color-code your chests using the paint function: green for crops, blue for fish, red for mining, yellow for artisan goods - Keep one chest outside near your farm for quick dump runs - Fridge in the kitchen for cooking ingredients only — saves trips to outdoor chests

    Tip: You can rename chests by interacting with them after placing. Use labels like "🌽 Crops" or "🐟 Fish" for quick visual scanning.

    💰 Cost-Efficient Comfort Strategy

    If you're on a budget, here's the fastest path from zero to a comfortable home:

    Phase 1 (0-25 Comfort, ~3,000g): - Basic bed + wood chair + small rug + potted plant - Enough for Wood Cabin tier bonuses

    Phase 2 (26-50 Comfort, ~10,000g): - Upgrade to Stone House - Comfort Bed + bookshelf + 2 plants + medium rug

    Phase 3 (51-80 Comfort, ~25,000g): - Brick House upgrade - Craft all cloth-based furniture (sofas, large rugs) - Aquarium + fish

    Phase 4 (81-120+, ~60,000g): - Manor/Mansion upgrade - Luxury Bed + chandelier + full wall decor - Every room filled

    Data source: Coral Island v1.1+ in-game data
