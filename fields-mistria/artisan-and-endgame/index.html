    🏭 Fields of Mistria — Complete Artisan Processing & Endgame Profit Model

    Data version: Fields of Mistria v1.0 (current) | Sources: Fields of Mistria Wiki, in-game testing | Last verified: 2026-07-06

    Fields of Mistria's economy runs on processing. A 20g potato becomes 130g preserves (+550%). A 60g tomato becomes 150g preserves (+150%). But unlike Stardew Valley where Kegs are the universal answer, Mistria forces you to make trade-offs — you only get one processing machine type per crop, and your Perk tree choices dramatically change which routes are optimal.

    This guide breaks down every processing machine, every crop's best route, and the optimal endgame setup for three player tiers.

    📖 Artisan Mechanics Overview

    Fields of Mistria has three main processing categories for crops:

    Category Machine Unlock Input Output Base Time
    🫙 Preserves Preserves Jar Town Rank 1 (Farming) Vegetables & Fruits Pickles / Jams ~6 hrs
    🥤 Juice Juice Press Town Rank 2 (Farming) Vegetables & Fruits Juices / Extracts ~4 hrs
    🥗 Cooking Kitchen (House Upgrade) Upgrade 2 Varied ingredients Prepared dishes Instant
    🧀 Animal Artisan Various Barn upgrades Milk, Eggs, Wool Cheese, Mayo, Cloth 3-6 hrs
    🎣 Fish Processing Smoker / Pickler Town Rank 3 Fish Smoked / Pickled Fish ~8 hrs

    Key difference from Stardew: Fields of Mistria's processing times are faster (hours, not days) and there is no Keg — the Juice Press is the closest equivalent. Also, cooking is genuinely profitable here unlike in most farm sims, because dishes can be sold for a multiple of their ingredient cost.

    🥤 Juice Press — Faster Turnover

    Unlocked at Town Rank 2 (Farming). The Juice Press processes in ~4 hours — faster than the Preserves Jar but with lower per-unit markup. Its key advantage: higher profit per hour despite lower markup, because it cycles faster.

    🏆 Best tesserae/hour in Juice Press: Watermelon (42.5g/h) — the fastest processing profit in the game per hour of machine time.

    Preserves Jar vs Juice Press — Which to Build?

    Crop Preserves Price Juice Price Advantage Verdict
    🥔 Potato 130g 120g Preserves +10g Jar
    🥕 Carrot 120g 115g Preserves +5g Jar
    🌽 Corn 165g 165g Equal Juice Press (faster)
    🥬 Kale 155g 155g Equal Juice Press (faster)
    🍉 Watermelon 340g 330g Preserves +10g Jar (better per unit)
    🍈 Melon 300g 290g Preserves +10g Jar
    🎃 Pumpkin 320g 305g Preserves +15g Jar

    General rule: For high-value crops (Watermelon, Melon, Pumpkin), use the Preserves Jar for higher per-unit profit. For mids and low-value crops (Corn, Kale, Carrot), the Juice Press cycles faster, giving similar or better profit/hour.

    🥗 Cooking Profit Analysis — Why It Matters Here

    Unlike Stardew Valley where cooking is strictly for buffs (never profit), Fields of Mistria has profitable cooking recipes. The Kitchen unlocks at House Upgrade 2, and certain dishes sell for 2-3× their ingredient cost.

    Top Profitable Dishes

    Dish Ingredients Ingredient Cost Sell Price Profit Multiplier
    🥗 Garden Salad Tomato + Lettuce 75g 200g +125g ×2.7
    🍲 Vegetable Stew Potato + Carrot + Onion 98g 280g +182g ×2.9
    🥟 Stuffed Cabbage Cabbage + Rice 120g 310g +190g ×2.6
    🍰 Berry Tart Strawberry×2 + Wheat Flour 200g 480g +280g ×2.4
    🥧 Pumpkin Pie Pumpkin + Wheat Flour + Egg 220g 550g +330g ×2.5
    🧀 Cheese Plate Cheese + Grape 180g 420g +240g ×2.3
    🍳 Farm Breakfast Egg + Potato + Milk 170g 400g +230g ×2.4
    🥘 Hearty Stew Meat + Potato + Carrot×2 220g 520g +300g ×2.4

    Key insight: Cooking can be more profitable per ingredient than artisan processing for some recipes. Vegetable Stew (2.9×) and Stuffed Cabbage (2.6×) beat most Preserves Jar routes. But cooking is limited by ingredient availability — you can't cook 200 Garden Salads if you only grow 50 tomatoes.

    When to Cook vs Process

    Scenario Better Route
    You have 5-10 high-value crops Preserves Jar (passive, stacks)
    You have 20+ low-value crops Juice Press (fast turnover)
    You have specific recipe ingredients Cook (higher multiplier, instant)
    Endgame with kitchen fully upgraded Cook + Process (divide your harvest)

    Optimal split: Process 60-70% of harvest through Preserves Jar (passive income), cook 20-30% for premium dishes, sell 10% raw for quick cash.

    🧀 Animal Artisan Goods

    Input Base Price Product Product Price Profit Multiplier Time

    🥚 Large Egg 50g Gold Mayo 180g +130g ×3.6 3h
    🥛 Milk 80g Cheese 200g +120g ×2.5 4h

    Never sell raw animal products. The multiplier is too good — even a basic Egg (30g → 110g Mayo) is +267% profit. Animal processing machines should be your first artisan infrastructure after basic crop processing.

    🌟 Perk Tree — The Hidden Profit Multiplier

    Fields of Mistria's Perk tree (unlocked via skill levels) is where the game's profit optimization gets deep. Different perk paths dramatically change your optimal strategy.

    Farming Perk Tree (Critical Nodes)

    Perk Level Effect Impact
    🫙 Preservation Expert Farming 3 Preserves Jar output +15% price 🟢 High
    🥤 Juice Master Farming 5 Juice Press output +15% price 🟢 High
    🧑‍🍳 Gourmet Cook Farming 7 Cooked dish sell price +20% 🟢 Very High
    🌿 Quality Harvest Farming 8 10% chance of double crop harvest 🟡 Medium
    🏆 Master Artisan Farming 10 All artisan goods +25% price 🔴 Essential

    Optimal Perk Path

    Early (Levels 1-4): Preservation Expert → Farming Efficiency
   ↳ Maximize Preserves Jar profit while machine count is low

Mid (Levels 5-7): Juice Master → Gourmet Cook
   ↳ Unlock Juice Press efficiency + cooking profitability

Late (Levels 8-10): Quality Harvest → Master Artisan
   ↳ Master Artisan is the single most powerful perk - +25% ALL artisan goods

    ⚠️ Master Artisan (+25%) is the endgame goal. It applies to Preserves Jar, Juice Press, AND animal artisan goods. Combined with Gourmet Cook (+20%), your Kitchen becomes a money printer.

    📐 Machine Ratio Optimization

    How many of each machine should you build? The answer depends on your farm size and season.

    Early Game (30-50 crop tiles)

    Machine Count Purpose
    🫙 Preserves Jar 3-4 Primary processing
    🥤 Juice Press 1-2 Fast turnover for mids
    🧀 Cheese Press 1 Animal products
    🫙 Mayo Machine 1 Egg processing
    🍳 Kitchen 1 Cook premium dishes

    Target: Process everything you harvest. Don't let crops sit.

    Mid Game (100-150 crop tiles)

    Machine Count Purpose
    🫙 Preserves Jar 8-12 Bulk processing
    🥤 Juice Press 4-6 Fast turnover
    🧀 Cheese Press 2-3 Animal products
    🫙 Mayo Machine 3-4 Egg processing
    🧥 Loom 1-2 Wool processing
    🍳 Kitchen 1 Gourmet cooking

    Target: 24-hour processing cycle. All daily harvests processed within the same game day.

    Endgame (250+ crop tiles, fully upgraded)

    Machine Count Purpose
    🫙 Preserves Jar 15-20 High-value crops
    🥤 Juice Press 8-12 Fast mids processing
    🧀 Cheese Press 4-6 Animal products
    🫙 Mayo Machine 6-8 Egg processing
    🧥 Loom 3-4 Wool processing
    🍳 Kitchen 1 Bulk premium cooking

    Target: All harvest processed. No raw crop ever touches the shipping bin.

    🎯 Player Tier Strategies

    Plan A: Zero-Spend / Minimal Savings (Starting Spring, ~500 Tesserae)

    Goal: Survive Spring, build 2 Preserves Jars, enter Summer with 5,000+ Tesserae.

    Spring Strategy:

    Week Focus Income Target
    1-2 Plant Carrots (cheap, fast). Sell raw for quick cash. Build 1 Preserves Jar. 800-1,200g
    3-4 Plant Cauliflower (highest spring profit/day). Process everything through the Jar. Sell raw surplus. 2,000-4,000g

    Summer Entry Checkpoint: - ✅ 2+ Preserves Jars built - ✅ ~3,000-5,000g in savings - ✅ Farming Perk: Preservation Expert unlocked

    Summer Strategy (Maximize Watermelon):

    Action Cost Income
    Plant 20 Watermelon (Day 1) -1,200g —
    Plant 15 Corn (Day 1) -525g —
    Day 10: First Watermelon harvest — +3,200g raw or +6,800g preserves
    Corn regrows every 3 days from Day 7 — +1,050g/harvest or +2,475g juice
    Replant Watermelon ×20 for wave 2 -1,200g +3,200g raw

    Summer Total (Zero-Spend): 15,000-25,000g net

    Plan B: Casual Player (Starting with ~5,000g savings)

    Goal: Balanced farm with diverse income. Build all machine types.

    Spring Setup:

    Action Cost
    15 Cauliflower (+ 15 Strawberry) seeds 750g + 900g
    3 Preserves Jars 450g materials
    1 Juice Press (Town Rank 2) 300g materials
    Chicken + Coop 1,500g

    Mid-Summer (After first big harvests):

    Income Source Weekly Tesserae
    Watermelon Preserves (20 tiles) ~6,800g/week
    Corn Juice (20 tiles) ~3,300g/week
    Mayo (3 chickens) ~1,540g/week
    Cooking (Vegetable Stew) ~2,000g/week
    Total ~13,640g/week

    Plan C: Speedrun / Optimizer (Starting with 20,000g+ savings or massive Spring)

    Goal: Full automation + maximum g/day by Fall Year 1.

    Summer Day 1 Mass Planting:

    Crop Tiles Seed Cost Strategy
    🍉 Watermelon 60 3,600g Preserves Jar (best per-unit)
    🌽 Corn 40 1,400g Juice Press (best per-hour)
    🍈 Melon 30 1,650g Preserves Jar (high value)
    🫐 Blueberry 30 1,200g Juice Press or Jam
    🍅 Tomato 20 600g Preserves or Cook (Salad)

    Infrastructure:

    Machine Count Total Cost
    🫙 Preserves Jar 12 ~1,800g
    🥤 Juice Press 8 ~1,200g
    🍳 Kitchen Upgrade 1 5,000g
    Mayo Machine 4 ~600g
    🧀 Cheese Press 2 ~400g
    Coop + Barn 1 each 4,000g

    Daily Income (Summer Peak):

    Source Daily
    Watermelon Preserves (60 tiles, wave cycles) 6,800g/day avg
    Corn Juice (40 tiles, every 3 days) 2,200g/day avg
    Melon Preserves (30 tiles) 2,400g/day avg
    Blueberry Juice (30 tiles) 1,750g/day avg
    Animal Products (processed) 1,200g/day
    Cooking (Garden Salad + Vegetable Stew) 2,500g/day
    Total Daily Income ~16,850g/day
    Season Total ~470,000-500,000g

    🏆 Endgame Maximum Profit Configuration

    Once you have Master Artisan (+25%) and Gourmet Cook (+20%), this is the optimal endgame farm:

    The Ultimate Preserves + Cooking Split

    Activity % of Harvest Machine Count Notes
    Preserves Jar 50% 20 Best for Watermelon, Pumpkin, Melon
    Juice Press 20% 10 Best for Corn, Blueberry
    Cooking 20% Kitchen Vegetable Stew, Berry Tart, Pumpkin Pie
    Animal Artisan 10% 12 total Mayo + Cheese + Loom

    Estimated Endgame Weekly Income (with Master Artisan + Gourmet Cook)

    Season Weekly Income Strategy
    🌸 Spring 50,000-70,000g Cauliflower Preserves + Strawberry Tart
    ☀️ Summer 100,000-150,000g Watermelon Preserves + Corn Juice + Vegetable Stew
    🍂 Fall 80,000-120,000g Pumpkin Pie + Cranberry Jam + Artichoke Preserves
    ❄️ Winter 15,000-25,000g Animal products + stored cooked goods

    Winter Tip: Stockpile cooked dishes in Fall. Pumpkin Pies and Vegetable Stews have long shelf life. Sell them in Winter when there's no fresh crop income.

    Year 1 Target Benchmarks

    Milestone Target Check
    End of Spring 5,000-10,000g Preserves Jar + basic animal setup
    End of Summer 50,000-100,000g Full machine setup + chickens/cows
    End of Fall 150,000-250,000g Master Artisan + Kitchen fully upgraded
    Year 1 Total 200,000-350,000g All upgrades + fully automated farm
