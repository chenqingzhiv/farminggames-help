    📋 Lease vs Buy — Cost Analysis

    Equipment Purchase Price Daily Rental Break-Even Point Verdict
    🚜 Medium Tractor (150 HP) $80,000 $1,500/day ~53 days Buy if using year-round
    🌾 Combine Harvester $250,000 $2,000/day ~125 days Rent — used only ~20 days/yr
    🧑‍🌾 Seeder (6m) $65,000 $800/day ~81 days Buy — used every planting
    🚛 Trailer (18,000L) $35,000 $400/day ~87 days Buy — essential daily use
    🪓 Forage Harvester $240,000 $1,800/day ~133 days Rent — seasonal specialist
    🪣 Sprayer (3,000L) $52,000 $600/day ~86 days Buy — essential for yields
    🧹 Mower (Hay) $25,000 $300/day ~83 days Buy if running animals

    Smart Rental Strategy

    Scenario Best Choice
    You have <$50K cash Rent everything — preserve capital for land
    You own a tractor + seeder Rent harvester 2×/year ($4,000) vs buying ($250,000)
    Doing one-off contracts Rent the specific tool the contract needs
    Expanding to animals Buy mower + baler; rent forage harvester
    3+ fields planted Time to buy your own harvester

    🗺️ Recommended Equipment Purchase Route

    Phase Budget What to Buy Why
    🔰 Hour 0–5 < $50K Rent everything. Buy a used Fliegl trailer ($17,500) Maximize capital for land
    🟢 Hour 5–20 $50–$150K Fendt 500 Vario ($85K) + Horsch Pronto ($65K) Core tractor + seeder combo
    🟡 Hour 20–50 $150–$300K John Deere 6R ($165K) OR Fendt 700 ($155K) + buy a used harvester Second tractor doubles speed
    🔶 Hour 50–100 $300–$500K Buy new combine (Case IH Axial-Flow $210K or New Holland CR $250K) Stop renting, keep full profit
    🔴 Hour 100+ $500K+ Case IH Steiger ($285K) + Fendt 1000 ($350K) + John Deere X9 ($375K) Heavy hitters for max scale

    Don't Buy These Too Early

    Item Why to Delay
    🚜 Heavy tractor (>300 HP) Implements are expensive too; pointless on small fields
    🌾 Forage harvester Only needed if running large silage operations
    🧑‍🌾 Precision farming equipment Requires GPS setup — mid-game investment
    🏗️ Biogas plant $500K — only after you have 50+ cows or 100+ acres
    🚁 Crop duster plane Fun but expensive; ground sprayers are more cost-effective
