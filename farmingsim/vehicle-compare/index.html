    Purchasing Tips

    1. Match tractor power to implement requirements — every tool has a minimum HP requirement. Undersized tractors work slowly; oversized tractors waste fuel.
    2. Consider fuel efficiency — Fendt tractors generally consume less fuel per HP/hour than competitors.
    3. Speed matters — a 50 km/h tractor covers fields faster than a 40 km/h model, saving real time on large maps.
    4. Don't overbuy — start with a medium tractor (140–150 HP) and a mid-range combine. Upgrade as your operation grows.
    5. Used vehicles — check the used vehicle dealer regularly for substantial discounts on older models.
    6. Lease before you buy — leasing a vehicle for a single contract is often cheaper than purchasing for infrequent use.
