# 🏠 Sun Haven — Housing & Decorating Guide

> Data version: Sun Haven 1.7 | Covers house upgrades, furniture, decorating, and the town building system

Your house in Sun Haven isn't just a place to sleep — it's a workshop hub, relationship booster, and the key to unlocking town upgrades. This guide covers everything from your first bed upgrade to endgame town restoration.

---

## 🏡 House Overview & Upgrades

Your starter home has 1 room, a single bed, and basic storage. Visit **Eli's Carpenter Shop** to upgrade.

| Upgrade | Cost (Tickets) | Materials | Rooms Added | Features |
|:--------|:--------------:|:----------|:-----------:|:---------|
| **1. Cozy Home** | 2,500 | 50 Wood, 30 Stone | +1 Bedroom | Bigger bed, wall decor slot |
| **2. Comfortable Home** | 5,000 | 80 Wood, 50 Stone, 10 Copper Bar | +1 Kitchen | Stove (cooking), 2nd chest slot |
| **3. Spacious Home** | 10,000 | 120 Wood, 80 Stone, 15 Iron Bar | +1 Living Room | Couch, bookshelf, fireplace slot |
| **4. Grand Home** | 20,000 | 200 Wood, 150 Stone, 20 Gold Bar | +1 Nursery | Child bed, pet bed slot |
| **5. Manor** | 40,000 | 300 Wood, 250 Stone, 30 Mithril Bar | +2 Rooms (attic + basement) | Auto-collect chest, teleport sigil |
| **6. Estate** | 80,000 | 500 Wood, 400 Stone, 50 Celestial Bar | +3 Rooms (wings) | Mana fountain, trophy hall |

> **Tip:** Upgrade to **Comfortable Home** (Lv 2) ASAP — having a kitchen lets you cook buff food without running to the town square. The Manor (Lv 5) is the first upgrade that pays for itself through the auto-collect chest.

## 🎨 Decorating Tips

### Room Scoring System

Your house has a **Deco Score** based on how many furniture pieces are placed in each room. Higher scores give relationship bonuses with NPCs who visit.

| Score | Rating | Bonus |
|:-----:|:-------|:------|
| 0–50 | 😐 Bare | No bonus |
| 51–150 | 🙂 Cozy | +2 friendship per visit |
| 151–300 | 😊 Beautiful | +4 friendship per visit |
| 301–500 | ❤️ Stunning | +6 friendship per visit, spouse bonus +50% |
| 500+ | ✨ Paradise | +10 friendship per visit, daily gift from spouse |

### Theme Ideas

| Theme | Colors | Key Furniture | Flooring |
|:------|:-------|:--------------|:---------|
| **Cozy Cabin** | Brown, Green, Warm Yellow | Wood furniture, fireplace, fur rug | Wood Floor |
| **Magic Study** | Purple, Blue, Silver | Bookshelves, crystal lamp, mana well | Celestial Tile |
| **Garden Room** | Green, Pink, White | Potted plants, fountain, glass walls | Stone Tile + Carpet |
| **Dark Manor** | Black, Red, Gold | Throne, tapestry, chandelier | Dark Marble |
| **Beach Retreat** | Sand, Blue, Coral | Shell decor, light wood, netting | Sandy Stone |

### Maximizing Deco Score Efficiently

The fastest way to hit **Stunning (301+)** without spending a fortune:

1. **Craft 10 Small Tables** (30 Wood total) — Place 2 per room
2. **Craft 10 Linen Curtains** (20 Fiber + 5 Wool) — One per window slot
3. **Buy 1 Chandelier** from Eli (800 Tickets) — Centerpiece for living room
4. **Craft Flooring** for every room — Bare floors lose points
5. **Add 1 Large Plant** per room — Magic Sapling (500 Tickets from Nel'Vari)

> **Tip:** Flooring and wallpaper give the most points per material cost. Always put flooring down before adding furniture.

## 🏘️ Town Building System

Sun Haven has a unique **Town Building** mechanic where you restore and renovate buildings around town. This is separate from your house.

### Renovatable Buildings

| Building | Cost | Materials | Reward |
|:---------|:----:|:----------|:-------|
| **Abandoned Barn** | 3,000 Tix | 100 Wood, 50 Stone | Unlocks second barn area |
| **Old Mill** | 5,000 Tix | 80 Wood, 30 Iron Bar | +20% flour production |
| **Broken Bridge** | 8,000 Tix | 200 Wood, 100 Stone | Connects to forest foraging area |
| **Ruined Tower** | 15,000 Tix | 150 Stone, 25 Gold Bar | Mana tower — +50 mana/day |
| **Abandoned Library** | 20,000 Tix | 300 Wood, 40 Mithril Bar | +2 skill books per week |
| **Town Square Fountain** | 30,000 Tix | 100 Stone, 50 Gold Bar | +1 heart to all NPCs permanently |
| **Farmers' Market Stalls** | 12,000 Tix | 80 Wood, 20 Iron Bar | +15% sell price at shop |
| **Wishing Well** | 10,000 Tix | 50 Stone, 10 Gold Bar | Daily random item (gems, ore, tickets) |

### Priority Order for Town Building

```
1. Broken Bridge → More foraging = more crafting materials
2. Abandoned Barn → Second barn for more animals
3. Town Square Fountain → Permanent +1 heart for ALL NPCs
4. Farmers' Market Stalls → +15% sell price (pays for itself)
5. Abandoned Library → Extra skill books per week
6. Ruined Tower → Passive mana generation
7. Old Mill → Nice but low priority
8. Wishing Well → Fun but random, lowest ROI
```

> **Tip:** The **Farmers' Market Stalls** (+15% sell price) and **Town Square Fountain** (+1 heart all NPCs) should be your first two major renovations after the Broken Bridge.

### Storage Room Layout (Manor Basement)

```
[Magic Chest] — Ore and bars
[Magic Chest] — Wood, stone, fiber
[Magic Chest] — Crafted items
[Magic Chest] — Seeds and crops
[Large Chest] — Food and meals
```

> **Tip:** Place Magic Chests around the map — one at your farm, one in the mines, one at the beach. Deposit ore directly at the mine entrance, withdraw bars at home. They're expensive but transform your workflow.

## 💡 Housing Tips & Tricks

1. **Upgrade to Comfortable Home first** — Having a kitchen at home saves 15+ minutes per day running to the town stove.
2. **Storage before decoration** — Build at least 4 Large Chests before buying a single decorative item. Organization pays.
3. **Bookshelf is worth every ticket** — An extra skill point every day adds up fast. Read the book immediately after buying.
4. **Magic Chests are endgame game-changers** — Save your Gold Bars for Magic Chests, not for selling. A network of 4 Magic Chests changes your daily routine.
5. **Town renovations stack** — The Farmers' Market bonus (+15% sell price) applies to everything you sell, including artisan goods. It's the best gold investment in town.
6. **Furniture can be picked up** — Unlike some games, you can freely move and rotate furniture without destroying it. Experiment!
7. **Theme bonuses don't stack** — You get one bonus per room, not per furniture type. A "Magic Study" theme gives +3 friendship per visit, not +3 per bookshelf.
