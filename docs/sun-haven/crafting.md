# 🔧 Sun Haven — Crafting & Artisan Guide

> Data version: Sun Haven 1.7 | Covers workbenches, artisan equipment, crafting recipes, and production chain optimization

Crafting in Sun Haven works differently than most farming sims. Instead of learning recipes from skill levels alone, you unlock new recipes through a combination of **skill progression**, **exploration drops**, and **quest rewards**. This guide breaks down every crafting station, recipe category, and the most profitable production chains.

---

## 🏗️ Crafting System Overview

Sun Haven has **five crafting stations**, each with its own recipe pool:

| Station | Cost | Unlock | Specialty |
|:--------|:-----|:-------|:----------|
| **Workbench** | 500 Tickets | Day 1 | Tools, furniture, basic goods |
| **Anvil** | 1,000 Tickets | Mining Lv 2 | Metal bars, weapons, armor |
| **Loom** | 800 Tickets | Farming Lv 3 | Cloth, thread, outfits |
| **Furnace** | 600 Tickets | Mining Lv 1 | Ore smelting, glass, bricks |
| **Cooking Station** | 400 Tickets | Foraging Lv 2 | Meals, buff food, dyes |

> **Tip:** Build all five stations before the end of Spring. The Workbench recipes alone won't carry you — each station gates essential progression items.

**Recipe sources:**
- **Skill level-ups** — Each skill unlocks specific recipes automatically
- **Chest loot** — Mines and dungeon chests contain rare recipe scrolls
- **Enemy drops** — Certain enemies drop crafting materials AND recipes
- **Vendors** — Eli's General Store and the Halloween Shop sell unique recipes
- **Quests** — Story and side quests reward exclusive schematics

## 🪚 Workbench — The Heart of Crafting

The Workbench is your first station and stays relevant all game. It covers **furniture, tools, storage, and general goods**.

### Essential Early Recipes

| Item | Materials | Use |
|:-----|:----------|:----|
| **Basic Storage Chest** | 25 Wood | 20-slot storage, stack to 99 |
| **Wooden Fence** | 5 Wood per section | Livestock containment |
| **Sprinkler (Basic)** | 2 Copper Bar + 5 Wood | Waters 3×3 area daily |
| **Scarecrow** | 20 Wood + 5 Fiber | Protects 10×10 area from crows |
| **Keg** | 30 Wood + 10 Copper Bar | Turns fruit into juice/wine |
| **Preserves Jar** | 20 Wood + 15 Stone | Turns veg into pickled goods |
| **Mayonnaise Machine** | 10 Wood + 8 Iron Bar | Eggs → Mayonnaise |
| **Cheese Press** | 15 Wood + 10 Iron Bar | Milk → Cheese |

### Workbench Upgrades

Your Workbench itself can be upgraded at the Carpenter for more recipe slots and faster crafting:

| Upgrade | Cost | Effect |
|:--------|:-----|:-------|
| Copper | 1,500 Tickets + 10 Copper Bar | +5 recipes, -10% craft time |
| Iron | 3,000 Tickets + 15 Iron Bar | +10 recipes, -20% craft time |
| Gold | 6,000 Tickets + 20 Gold Bar | +15 recipes, -30% craft time |
| Mithril | 12,000 Tickets + 25 Mithril Bar | +20 recipes, -40% craft time, auto-craft queue |

> **Tip:** The Gold Workbench is the sweet spot. Mithril is expensive and the auto-craft queue mostly matters if you're mass-producing for town orders.

## 🧪 Artisan Equipment — Production Chains

Artisan equipment turns raw farm products into **high-value processed goods**. This is where the real money is.

### Keg vs Preserves Jar vs Juice Press

| Machine | Cost | Input | Output | Base Price | Time |
|:--------|:-----|:------|:-------|:----------:|:----:|
| **Keg** | 30 Wood + 10 Cu Bar | Fruit | Wine/Juice | 3× base | 2 days |
| **Preserves Jar** | 20 Wood + 15 Stone | Veg/Fruit | Pickled/Jam | 2.5× base | 1 day |
| **Juice Press** | 25 Wood + 8 Iron Bar | Any crop | Juice | 1.75× base | 12 hrs |

### Best Artisan Chains by Season

| Season | Crop | Process | Sell Price | Gold/Day | Machine |
|:-------|:-----|:--------|:----------:|:--------:|:--------|
| Spring | Strawberry → Wine | Keg | 360g | 180g/day | Keg |
| Spring | Tomato → Pickled | Preserves | 210g | 210g/day | Preserves Jar |
| Summer | Blueberry → Wine | Keg | 150g | 75g/day | Keg |
| Summer | Corn → Pickled | Preserves | 150g | 150g/day | Preserves Jar |
| Fall | Pumpkin → Pickled | Preserves | 790g | 790g/day | Preserves Jar |
| Fall | Grape → Wine | Keg | 240g | 120g/day | Keg |
| Winter | Crystal Fruit → Juice | Juice Press | 270g | 540g/day | Juice Press |

### Artisan Animal Products

| Raw | Machine | Output | Sell Price | Multiplier |
|:----|:--------|:-------|:----------:|:----------:|
| Egg (15g) | Mayo Machine | Mayonnaise | 60g | **4.0×** |
| Milk (25g) | Cheese Press | Cheese | 110g | **4.4×** |
| Goat Milk (30g) | Cheese Press | Goat Cheese | 140g | **4.7×** |
| Wool (45g) | Loom | Cloth | 160g | **3.6×** |
| Unicorn Milk (120g) | Cheese Press | Unicorn Cheese | 520g | **4.3×** |

> **Tip:** Preserves Jar beats Keg for short-season crops. A Pumpkin (320g) turned into Pickled Pumpkin (790g) in 1 day is 790g/day — while a Grape turned into Wine (240g) over 2 days is only 120g/day.

## ⛓️ Best Production Chains for Profit

### Early Game Chain (Spring, 5,000–10,000 Ticket budget)

```
Buy Chickens (30 Tix each)
→ Collect Eggs daily
→ Mayo Machine (800 Tix)
→ Sell Mayonnaise (60g each)
→ Reinvest into more Chickens + Kegs

Daily profit with 8 Chickens + 2 Mayo Machines: ~480g/day
```

### Mid-Game Chain (Summer, 15,000–30,000 Ticket budget)

```
Strawberries + Blueberries planted
→ Keg × 10 (1,000 Tix total)
→ Wine production every 2 days
→ Plus Coop animals + Mayo

Daily profit: ~2,500g/day
```

### Late-Game Chain (Endgame, 100,000+ Ticket budget)

```
Unicorn (500 Tix) × 4
→ Cheese Press × 4 (4,800 Tix)
→ Unicorn Cheese (520g each, daily)
→ Plus 20+ Kegs running Crystal Fruit Wine (Winter)
→ Or mass Pumpkin Pickled via Preserves (Fall)

Daily profit: ~10,000g+/day
```

### Withergate Zone Production

| Crop | Process | Profit/Week |
|:-----|:--------|:-----------:|
| Void Tomato → Pickled | Preserves Jar | 4,200g |
| Shadow Berry → Wine | Keg | 3,600g |
| Nether Wheat → Flour (no machine needed) | Sold raw | 1,800g |

> **Tip:** Nel'Vari crops process into higher-tier goods. Shadow Berry Wine sells for 1.5× normal wine prices due to the magic crop bonus.

## 📈 Crafting Skill Progression

Your **Crafting skill** levels up as you create items at any station.

| Level | Unlock | Bonus |
|:-----:|:-------|:------|
| 1 | Basic Workbench recipes | None |
| 2 | Copper-tier Anvil recipes | -5% material cost |
| 3 | Loom recipes unlocked | +10% craft speed |
| 4 | Keg recipe | -10% material cost |
| 5 | Preserves Jar recipe | +15% craft speed |
| 6 | Iron-tier Anvil recipes | Unlock quality crafting |
| 7 | Juice Press recipe | -15% material cost |
| 8 | Gold-tier upgrade | +10% quality chance |
| 9 | Mithril-tier recipes | -20% material cost |
| 10 | Celestial-tier recipes | +25% quality chance, auto-craft queue |

**Fastest way to level Crafting:** Mass-produce **Rope** (5 Fiber) at the Loom. It's cheap, fast (1 hour), and gives the same XP as a complex item. 100 Rope will take you from Lv 1 to Lv 8 in about 3 in-game days.

## 💡 Crafting Tips & Tricks

1. **Always have a Preserves Jar and Keg running** — Even if it's just a single crop, idle artisan machines are lost money.
2. **Keep a material stockpile** — 50 Wood, 30 Stone, 20 Fiber, and 10 of each bar type. Nothing worse than finding a new recipe and not having the mats.
3. **Recipe Scrolls from mining** — Every 5th floor in the Sun Haven mines has a guaranteed recipe chest. Don't skip them.
4. **Furniture crafting XP is lower** — Furniture gives 60% of the XP of a functional item. Use furniture for aesthetics, not grinding.
5. **Auto-craft queue (Mithril Workbench)** — This is amazing for bulk Preserves Jar runs. Queue up 50 Pickled Pumpkins and forget about it for a week.
6. **Sell raw vs processed** — Raw crops sell for 1×, processed sells for 2–4×. Never sell raw animal products once you have the machine.
7. **Nel'Vari has unique recipes** — Visit the Nel'Vari crafting vendor for recipes you can't get anywhere else, including the Mana Infuser.
