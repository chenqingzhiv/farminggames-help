## 🔥 How Cooking Works

Cooking in Core Keeper is a **crafting activity** that transforms raw ingredients into meals with bonus effects. Unlike campfire cooking (which just restores HP), the **Cooking Pot** and **Advanced Cooking Station** produce food with **temporary buffs** and **greatly boosted sell prices**.

### Key Mechanics

| Mechanic | Detail |
|:---------|:-------|
| **Unlock** | Cooking Pot blueprint drops from **Ghorm the Devourer** (or found in Forgotten Ruins chests) |
| **Station** | Craft at a Workbench: 20 Copper Bars + 10 Tin Bars + 8 Fibre |
| **Fuel** | No fuel needed — recipes are instant crafts at the station |
| **Ingredients** | Crops, fish, meat, mushrooms, and foraged items |
| **Output** | Always produces 1 dish per recipe (some yield 2 with Gardening Mastery) |
| **Buffs** | Most cooked dishes grant a 5–10 minute stat buff + HP recovery |
| **Buff Stacking** | Eating the same dish refreshes duration; different dish buffs run on separate timers |

> **💡 Cooking Pot vs Campfire:** The basic Campfire (unlocked at start) can only **roast** individual items for minor HP gain. The **Cooking Pot** and **Advanced Cooking Station** are required for proper recipes with buffs.

## 🏗️ Cooking Stations

| Station | Materials | Unlock | Features |
|:--------|:---------|:-------|:---------|
| 🔥 **Campfire** | 10 Wood, 5 Fibre | Starting recipe | Roasts single items (minimal benefit) |
| 🥘 **Cooking Pot** | 20 Copper Bar, 10 Tin Bar, 8 Fibre | Ghorm drop / Ruins chest | All basic recipes, crop + fish dishes |
| ⚗️ **Advanced Cooking Station** | 15 Iron Bar, 10 Gold Bar, 5 Scarlet Bar | Late-game (Scarlet biome) | Advanced recipes, longer buff durations |

### Where to Find the Cooking Pot

The Cooking Pot recipe is obtained by:

1. **Defeat Ghorm the Devourer** — drops the blueprint ~80% of the time
2. **Forgotten Ruins chests** — check the wooden chests in the maze-like ruins
3. **Scrap Merchant** — sometimes sells it for 150 coins

Once you have the blueprint, craft it at a Workbench and place it in your base.

## 📊 Food Buff Mechanics Explained

### How Food Buffs Work

1. **Duration:** Most food buffs last **5–8 minutes** (advanced dishes up to 15 min)
2. **Stacking:** Eating the same dish again **refreshes** the duration (does not stack intensity)
3. **Separate Timers:** Different buff types (Move Speed, Mining Speed, etc.) run independently
4. **Food Slot:** Eating any food gives a **temporary buff icon** in your HUD — you can see remaining duration
5. **Overheal:** Food HP recovery can overheal you beyond your max HP (up to ~150% of max)
6. **Cancel:** Buffs are cancelled on death (food effects do NOT persist through respawn)

### Buff Types Reference

| Buff Type | Effect | Best Source | Max Tier Available |
|:----------|:-------|:-----------|:------------------|
| ❤️ **Max HP** | +20 to +25% max health | Mushroom Risotto, Hearty Roast, Defender's Platter | +25% |
| 💚 **HP Regen** | X HP per second | Salad series, Mushroom Medley Supreme | +6 HP/s |
| ⛏️ **Mining Speed** | +10% to +30% mine speed | Spicy Wrap, Miner's Banquet | +30% |
| 🏃 **Move Speed** | +8% to +20% movement | Coffee, Pineapple Juice | +20% |
| ⚔️ **Melee Damage** | +8% to +25% melee | Hot Pepper Stew, Hunter's Stew | +25% |
| 🛡️ **Defence** | +5% to +25% damage reduction | Pumpkin Pie, Defender's Platter | +25% |
| 🌊 **Breathing** | Enables underwater breathing | Glow Tuna Steak | Duration only |
| 💡 **Light** | +2 to +3 tiles light radius | Glowing Broth, Glowing Mushroom Stew | +3 tiles |
| 🍀 **Luck** | +10% loot luck | Mirage Soup | +10% |
| 🎣 **Fishing Power** | +20% catch rate | Fisherman's Platter | +20% |
| 🎲 **Dodge** | +10% dodge chance | Grape Salad | +10% |
| 🌟 **All Stats** | +30% to everything | Ambrosia (endgame legendary) | +30% |

### Buff Stacking Rules

```
✅ Move Speed (Coffee) + Mining Speed (Spicy Wrap) → BOTH active, separate timers
✅ Melee Damage (Hot Pepper Stew) + Max HP (Mushroom Risotto) → BOTH active
✅ Multiple Move Speed foods → Only highest Move Speed buff applies
❌ Same buff type from different foods → Only the strongest buff active
❌ Same dish eaten twice → Refreshes timer, does not double the buff
❌ Food buff + same buff from gear → They DO stack (e.g. Coffee + Swift Ring = big speed)
```

> **💡 Key Insight:** Buff effects from **food + gear** stack additively. A Coffee (+15% Move Speed) plus a Swift Ring (+10% Move Speed) gives you **+25% total**. Always combine food buffs with appropriate gear for maximum effect.

### Quick Tips

- **Always carry Bread** — cheap, stacks well, decent heal
- **Coffee is worth farming** — 5 coffee bushes = infinite speed buff
- **Cook fish, sell crops** — opposite of what you might expect
- **Different buffs stack** — stack Mining Speed + Move Speed for efficient resource runs
- **Gear + food stacks** — double down on your build's main stat
- **Ambrosia is endgame** — save the rare ingredients (King Pepper, Amber Pumpkin) for bosses
