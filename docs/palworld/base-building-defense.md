## 🏗️ Base Building Fundamentals

### How Bases Work

In Palworld, a **base** is centred around your **Pal Box** (unlocked at Level 2). The Pal Box defines your base's radius (roughly 30 tiles in diameter) and is where all base operations are managed.

| Base Mechanic | Detail |
|:--------------|:-------|
| **Max Bases** | 3 bases per world (default setting) |
| **Pal Capacity** | Starts at 5 Pals/base, increases with Pal Box level (max ~20) |
| **Base Radius** | ~30 tiles from Pal Box (roughly 60×60 tiles total) |
| **Building Limit** | 3,000 structures total across all bases |
| **Power** | Electricity unlocks at Level 35+ via Generator + Orserk |
| **Teleport** | Fast travel to any Pal Box from a fast travel point |

> **💡 Pal Box leveling:** Upgrade your Pal Box by spending resources (wood, stone, ingots, etc.) at the box itself. Each level increases Pal capacity, base radius, and unlocks new buildable structures.

### The Three-Base Strategy

With 3 bases available, optimise each for a different purpose:

| Base # | Purpose | Best Location | Key Structures |
|:-------|:--------|:--------------|:---------------|
| 🏠 **Base 1** | Production & Housing | Flat, central, near water | All workstations, farms, crafting |
| ⛏️ **Base 2** | Mining & Resources | Ore-rich cliff or mountain | Mining Pits, Crusher, Smelter |
| 🧬 **Base 3** | Breeding & Ranching | Peaceful, flat, no raids | Breeding Farm, Ranch, Hatchery |

> **⚠️ Important:** Base 1 will face the most raid attacks. Build your defences here first. Base 2 and 3 can be in lower-risk areas.

## 🗺️ Choosing Base Locations

### Production Base (Base 1) Location Criteria

| Factor | Ideal | Avoid |
|:-------|:------|:------|
| 🔹 **Flatness** | Large flat area (no cliffs) | Rocky terrain, steep slopes |
| 💧 **Water access** | River or pond nearby for Watering Pals | Desert (no water) |
| 🌲 **Resources** | Near trees + ore + stone | Middle of nowhere |
| 🗺️ **Fast travel** | Within running distance of a node | Far from any travel point |
| 🛡️ **Raid safety** | Natural chokepoint | Open plains (raids come from all sides) |

           │                            │
        [Ore Pit]                    [Ore Pit]

| 1 | ⛏️ Mining | Astegon (Lv4) | Fastest ore breaker |
| 2 | ⛏️ Mining | Blazamut (Lv4) | Also provides Kindling 3 |

## 🛡️ Raid Defense Systems

### How Raids Work

Raids are attacks on your base by hostile NPCs (Syndicate Thugs, Free Pal Alliance) or wild Pals. They trigger when:

- Your base's **total defence score** is below the raid threshold
- You accumulate **wanted levels** (killing humans in towns)
- Raiders spawn at the edge of your base radius and move toward your Pal Box

| Raid Type | Enemies | Attack Style | Difficulty |
|:-----------|:--------|:-------------|:-----------|
| 👤 **Syndicate Thugs** | 5–15 humans | Ranged + melee | Easy |
| 🐾 **Wild Pals** | 5–10 Pals | Elemental attacks | Medium |
| 🏰 **Free Pal Alliance** | 8–12 humans + Pals | Coordinated attacks | Hard |
| 🐉 **Raid Bosses** | 1 giant Pal + waves | Boss mechanics | **Extreme** |

### Defense Wall Design

Building proper walls is **essential** for surviving late-game raids. Here's the proven design:

```
Top View:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  🧱 Layer 3: Pal Box (innermost)            │
│     ┌─────────────────────────┐             │
│     │  🧱 Layer 2: Workstations│             │
│     │     ┌───────────────┐   │             │
│     │     │  🧱 Layer 1:    │   │             │
│     │     │  Weapon Posts  │   │             │
│     │     └───────────────┘   │             │
│     └─────────────────────────┘             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Wall Building Materials by Stage

| Material | HP | Build Cost | Best For |
|:---------|:---|:-----------|:---------|
| 🪵 **Wood** | 500 | 5 Wood | Early game (temporary) |
| 🪨 **Stone** | 1,200 | 5 Stone | Mid-game (minimum for raids) |
| 🧱 **Brick** | 2,000 | 5 Brick | Standard defence |
| 🔩 **Metal** | 3,500 | 5 Ingot | **Late-game standard** |
| 🏗️ **Reinforced Concrete** | 5,000 | 5 Cement + 2 Ingot | **Endgame fortress** |

> **⚠️ Never use wood walls.** Wood walls are destroyed in 2–3 hits by mid-game raiders. Rush to stone, then metal as fast as possible.

### Defense Post Layout

The most efficient defense layout uses **Defense Posts** (unlocked at Level 30):

```
Layout:
[Wall] [Wall] [Defense Post] [Wall] [Wall]
                 ↑
            Gate (open for Pals)
                 ↑
[Wall] [Wall] [Defense Post] [Wall] [Wall]
```

| Defense Post | Materials | Damage | Ammo | Best Against |
|:-------------|:----------|:-------|:-----|:-------------|
| 🏹 **Arrow Post** | 30 Wood + 10 Stone | Low | Arrows | Early game |
| 🔫 **Gun Post** | 50 Ingot + 20 Circuit | Medium | Bullets | Mid-game |
| ⚡ **Laser Post** | 100 Ingot + 50 Circuit + 10 Polymer | **High** | Power | **Endgame** |

> **💡 Automation tip:** Assign a Pal with Handiwork to Defense Posts. They will automatically man the post and fire when raiders approach. No player interaction needed.

### Chokepoint Strategy

Don't surround your entire base with walls — it wastes materials and spreads your defense too thin. Instead:

1. **Identify attack directions** — raids always come from the same direction based on your map location
2. **Build a V-shaped killbox** pointing toward the attack direction
3. **Fill the killbox with Defense Posts** (4–6 posts)
4. **Leave your other sides open** (Pals can't path through walls, so open sides let them escape danger)

```
           R A I D E R   S P A W N
                    │
                    ▼
              ┌──────────┐
              │  KILL BOX  │ ← Defense Posts here
              └────┬─────┘
                   │
             [Base Entrance]
                   │
            ┌──────┴──────┐
            │  YOUR BASE  │
            └─────────────┘
```

## 🐉 Raid Boss Guide: Bellanoir

### Unlocking Bellanoir

Bellanoir is the first raid boss, added in the **Sakurajima Update**.

| Requirement | Detail |
|:------------|:-------|
| **Level** | 40+ recommended |
| **Summon Item** | Bellanoir Slab (crafted at Pal Workbench) |
| **Slab Materials** | 50 Paldium Fragment + 20 Bone + 10 Large Pal Soul |
| **Location** | Summon at **Summoning Altar** (unlocked at Level 35) |
| **Summoning Altar** | 100 Stone + 50 Wood + 20 Ingot |

### Boss Stats

| Stat | Bellanoir | Bellanoir Libero (Hard Mode) |
|:-----|:----------|:----------------------------|
| **HP** | ~50,000 | ~150,000 |
| **Element** | Dark | Dark (enraged) |
| **Attacks** | Dark orbs, teleport, AoE darkness | All + laser beam + meteor |
| **Minions** | Dark Pals (Lv30) | Dark Pals (Lv45) |
| **Phase 2 at** | 50% HP | 70% HP |
| **Timer** | 10 min | 15 min |

### Recommended Setup

| Category | Setup |
|:---------|:------|
| 🐉 **Your Pals** | 5× Dragon-type (Jormuntide, Jetragon, Orserk) |
| ⚔️ **Weapon** | Rocket Launcher + Assault Rifle (Legendary) |
| 🛡️ **Armour** | Pal Metal Armour (minimum) or Hexolite |
| 🍽️ **Food** | Pizza (+Defence) + Strong Medicine (+Attack) |
| 🔄 **Strategy** | Kite Bellanoir in a circle, fire rockets, call Pals back when low HP |

### Bellanoir Fight Phases

**Phase 1 (100%–50% HP):**
- Bellanoir teleports around the arena
- Fires slow-moving dark orbs — dodge sideways
- Occasionally summons Dark Pals — kill them quickly (they heal Bellanoir if left alive)

**Phase 2 (50%–0% HP):**
- Dark vortex AoE in centre — stay at range
- More frequent teleports
- Enraged mode: all attacks +50% damage
- **Key strategy:** Use Dragon Pals (250% damage to Dark type). Assign all 5 Pals to aggressive mode.

> **💡 Bellanoir Libero (Hard):** Bring 3× the healing items, use the Legendary Rocket Launcher, and consider bringing a **mounted** Pal (Jetragon) for mobility. Libero's laser beam is a one-hit-kill — always keep moving.

## 🐉 Raid Boss Guide: Xenolord

### Unlocking Xenolord

Xenolord is the **hardest raid boss**, added in the **Feybreak Update**.

| Requirement | Detail |
|:------------|:-------|
| **Level** | 55+ recommended (level cap is 60) |
| **Summon Item** | Xenolord Slab |
| **Slab Materials** | 200 Paldium Fragment + 50 Hexolite + 30 Large Pal Soul + 10 Ancient Technical Parts |
| **Location** | Summoning Altar (must be upgraded to Level 3) |
| **Warning** | Prepare to lose your base — Xenolord destroys structures |

### Boss Stats

| Stat | Xenolord | Xenolord (Ultra) |
|:-----|:---------|:-----------------|
| **HP** | ~200,000 | ~500,000 |
| **Element** | Dark + Dragon | Dark + Dragon (enraged) |
| **Attacks** | Void beam, dimensional tear, gravity well | All + nova explosion |
| **Minions** | Dark + Dragon Pals (Lv 50+) | Ultra versions |
| **Phase 2 at** | 70% HP | 90% HP |

### Dedicated Raid Base Strategy

Unlike Bellanoir and Blazamut Ryu, Xenolord **will destroy your base** unless you build a dedicated raid arena:

**How to build a raid arena (not your main base):**
1. Place a temporary **Pal Box** in a flat area far from your main base
2. Build the Summoning Altar at this location
3. Surround the altar with **Reinforced Concrete walls** (5,000 HP each)
4. Place Defense Posts on the outer ring
5. Summon Xenolord here — let the arena take the damage

### Recommended Setup

| Category | Setup |
|:---------|:------|
| 🐉 **Your Pals** | 5× Dragon-type (Jetragon, Orserk) + Dark-resistant |
| ⚔️ **Weapon** | Legendary Plasma Cannon + Hexolite Shotgun |
| 🛡️ **Armour** | Hexolite Armour (max upgraded) |
| 💊 **Healing** | 100× High-Grade Medicine + 10× Revival Potions |
| 🔋 **Accessories** | Electric Shield + Gravity Gloves |

### Fight Strategy

**Phase 1 (100%–70% HP):**
- Xenolord opens with Void Beam — a continuous laser sweep
- Run perpendicular to the beam direction
- Dimensional Tear creates portals that spawn minions
- Stay on a flying mount (Jetragon) to avoid ground AoE

**Phase 2 (70%–0% HP):**
- Gravity Well pulls you toward Xenolord — spam dodge roll to escape
- Nova explosion at 30% and 10% HP — run to the arena edge
- **Final enrage** (10%): Xenolord's attacks are 300% damage. This is a DPS race.

> **💡 Team composition for Ultra:** Bring 4× max-condensed Jetragon with Holy Burst (+Divine Dragon) for massive damage. Bring 1× Lyleen with Healing Wave to keep your Pals alive. The healing Pal makes a bigger difference than a 5th damage dealer.

## 🏝️ Feybreak Island Endgame

Feybreak Island was added in the **Feybreak Update (December 2024)** and represents Palworld's hardest content.

### Key Differences

| Feature | Base Game | Feybreak Island |
|:---------|:----------|:----------------|
| **Level cap** | 50 | **60** (unlock via Feybreak XP) |
| **New resource** | — | **Hexolite** (crafting + gear) |
| **New Pals** | — | 30+ new Pals (Xenolord, Feybreak variants) |
| **New gear** | — | Hexolite Armour, Plasma Cannon, Gravity Gloves |
| **New mechanics** | — | Meteor Events, Supply Drops, World Bosses |
| **Difficulty** | Standard | Extreme (all mobs +20 levels) |

### How to Reach Feybreak

1. Reach **Level 45+** in the base game
2. Defeat at least one Tower Boss (Victor & Shadowbeak recommended)
3. Travel to the **Southwest corner** of the map
4. Cross the **Feybreak Bridge** (guarded by Level 55 Pals)
5. Build a **small forward base** immediately upon arrival (no fast travel otherwise)

### Feybreak Essential Gear Progression

| Stage | Gear | Materials Needed |
|:------|:-----|:----------------|
| 🥇 **Arrival** | Pal Metal Armour (base) | Existing base resources |
| 🥈 **30 min** | Hexolite Pickaxe | 15 Hexolite + 10 Paldium |
| 🥉 **1 hour** | Hexolite Armour | 60 Hexolite + 30 Pal Metal Ingot |
| 🏆 **Endgame** | Plasma Cannon | 30 Hexolite + 20 Technical Parts + 5 Xenolord Soul |

### Feybreak Key Resource: Hexolite

Hexolite is Feybreak's signature resource. It appears as **glowing purple crystal deposits** on cliffsides.

| Hexolite Tool | Materials | Effect |
|:--------------|:----------|:-------|
| 🪓 **Hexolite Pickaxe** | 15 Hexolite + 10 Paldium | Mining speed +200% |
| 🪚 **Hexolite Axe** | 15 Hexolite + 10 Wood | Lumbering speed +200% |
| ⛏️ **Hexolite Drill** | 30 Hexolite + 20 Circuit | Automated mining (very fast) |

> **💡 Hexolite farming tip:** Set up a mining base on Feybreak with 3× Hexolite-capable Pals (new Feybreak Pals have innate Hexolite detection). Each deposit respawns every ~15 in-game minutes. A dedicated mining base here is incredibly profitable.

## 🛢️ Oil Rig & Crude Oil

Crude Oil nodes appear in two locations:

Oil is extracted using **Oil Extractors** (unlocked at Level 45):

| Extractor | Materials | Output | Power |
|:----------|:----------|:-------|:------|

### What You Need Oil For

| Item | Oil Cost | Why You Need It |

## 🧬 Pal Condensation & IV Optimisation

### What Is Condensation?

Condensation is Palworld's **endgame Pal power-up system**, unlocked at Level 20+ via the **Pal Condenser**.

| Condense Level | Pals Required | Stat Bonus | Skill Upgrade |
|:--------------|:--------------|:-----------|:--------------|
| ⭐ **Star 1** | 4 Pals | +5% all stats | Partner Skill Lv 2 |
| ⭐ **Star 2** | 20 Pals (16 more) | +10% all stats | Partner Skill Lv 3 |
| ⭐ **Star 3** | 50 Pals (30 more) | +15% all stats | Partner Skill Lv 4 |
| ⭐ **Star 4** | 100 Pals (50 more) | +20% all stats | Partner Skill Lv 5 |

> **💡 Condensation Cost:** To fully max a single Pal from 0 to 4 stars = **100 Pals of the same species**. This is a massive grind — prioritise your rarest and most useful Pals first.

### IV System

Pals have **hidden IVs** (Individual Values) that determine their stat growth:

| Stat | IV Range | Effect at Max | How to Improve |
|:-----|:---------|:--------------|:---------------|
| ❤️ **HP IV** | 0–100 | +30% max HP | Breeding |
| ⚔️ **Attack IV** | 0–100 | +30% attack | Breeding |
| 🛡️ **Defence IV** | 0–100 | +30% defence | Breeding |
| 🏃 **Speed IV** | 0–100 | +30% speed | Catching specific Pals |

**How to check IVs:** Use a mod (like the IV-UI mod) or use the **Pal Trader** NPC on Feybreak (he assesses your Pals' potential).

**IV Breeding Strategy:**
1. Catch 100+ of the Pal you want to perfect
2. Check their IVs (via mod or Feybreak NPC)
3. Keep any Pal with 80+ in one stat
4. Breed the best HP with best Attack → hatch offspring with combined IVs
5. Repeat until you have a Pal with 90+ in all 3 combat stats

> **💡 IV vs Condensation:** Condensation gives +20% to all stats. IV gives +30% to one specific stat. Both matter for endgame. Prioritise Condensation for base workers, IV for combat Pals.

## 🗿 Statue of Power & Pal Souls

### How It Works

The **Statue of Power** (unlocked at Level 10) lets you permanently upgrade your Pals using **Pal Souls** — rare drops from bosses, chests, and dungeons.

| Statue Level | Pal Soul Cost Per Upgrade | Max Upgrades Per Stat |
|:-------------|:-------------------------|:---------------------|
| 1 (Base) | 1 Small Soul | 4 |
| 2 (Upgraded) | 1 Medium Soul | 8 |
| 3 (Max) | 1 Large Soul | 14 |

### Pal Soul Types

| Soul Type | Best Source | Used For |
|:----------|:-----------|:---------|
| 🔵 **Small Pal Soul** | Dungeon bosses, chests | Early upgrades (HP, Attack, Defence) |
| 🟣 **Medium Pal Soul** | Tower bosses, Dungeon rewards | Mid upgrades |
| 🟠 **Large Pal Soul** | **Raid bosses** (Bellanoir, Blazamut Ryu, Xenolord) | Endgame upgrades |

### Upgrade Priority

| Stat | Why | Base Pals | Combat Pals |
|:-----|:----|:----------|:------------|
| ❤️ **HP** | Survival against raids | 🥇 | 🥇 |
| ⚔️ **Attack** | Damage output | 🥈 | 🥇 |
| 🛡️ **Defence** | Reduced damage taken | 🥉 | 🥈 |
| 🏃 **Work Speed** | Faster base production | 🥇 base workers | ❌ ignore |

> **⚠️ Large Souls are the bottleneck.** You get ~3 per raid boss kill. A single Pal needs 14 Large Souls for max upgrades. You'll need to farm Bellanoir ~5 times per Pal.

## 💡 Pro Base Building Tips

### 1. The "Foundation First" Rule
Always build a **foundation** (floor tiles) before walls. Pals can't path well on sloped terrain. A flat foundation prevents pathing issues.

### 2. Ceilings Are Free Space
Build a **second floor** for structures that don't need ground access:
- Floor 1: Workstations, farms, crafting
- Floor 2: Chests, beds, decor
- Roof: Pal Statue, solar panels

### 3. Stairs Are One-Wide
A single staircase is enough for base access. Pals path one-at-a-time through 1-tile gaps. Wider stairs don't help — but a **second staircase** on the opposite side does.

### 4. The 2-Tile Door Trick
Place doors in **2-tile gaps** in your walls. Pals path through open doors, but larger enemies (like raid bosses) can't fit through. Forces enemies to attack walls instead of sprinting to your Pal Box.

### 5. Pal Bed Distribution
Place beds **near workstations**, not all in one bedroom. Pals sleep when their SAN drops to 0 and wake up wherever they are. Having beds distributed reduces travel time.

### 6. Hot Spring × 2 Minimum
A single Hot Spring can serve 10 Pals if placed centrally. With 15+ Pals, build 2 Hot Springs on opposite sides of the base. Pal SAN management is the #1 bottleneck in large bases.

### 7. Don't Build on Ore Nodes
If you build a structure on top of an ore node, the node disappears permanently. Build **around** ore clusters, not on them.

### 8. Repair Between Raids
Always repair walls and Defense Posts between raid waves. A single gap in your defense line can cascade into a full base breach.

### 9. Save Schematics
Use the **Blueprint function** (available after Level 40) to save productive base layouts. You can then rebuild them at a second base in seconds.

### 10. Three Bases = Three Specializations
Never build three identical bases. This is the most common mistake in Palworld:
- Base 1: Production + Defense (the "main" base)
- Base 2: Resource mining (passive income)
- Base 3: Breeding + Ranching (specialist production)
