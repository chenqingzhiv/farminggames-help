## 🌾 Meadowlands Farm

**Meadowlands Farm** is a brand-new farm map introduced in 1.6, designed for players who want a head start in animal husbandry.

### Key Features

| Feature | Detail |
|:--------|:-------|
| **Starting Animals** | A coop with **2 chickens** already built |
| **Starting Feed** | A **hay silo** and **lots of grass starters** |
| **Green Tile** | The **green grass tiles (blue on minimap)** regrow overnight — you can **scythe them for hay** every day |
| **Water Source** | A small pond in the southwest |
| **Debris** | Less tree debris, more grass — ideal for grazing |
| **Farm Type Bonuses** | **Cheaper animal products** (profession bonus applies earlier) |

### Strategy Notes

- You start with an edge in **animal income** — focus on mayo/cheese early.
- The green tiles provide **~20 hay/day** from scything, supplementing grass starters.
- The blue grass tiles also feed animals directly without needing to cut it.
- Great for **Ranching** and **Artisan** profession paths.
- However: **less crop space** than Standard Farm — plan your layout around animals.

> **Verdict:** The best farm for new players who want to focus on animals without the steep early cost of coops and silos.

**Source:** [Stardew Valley Wiki - Meadowlands Farm](https://stardewvalleywiki.com/Meadowlands_Farm)

## 🌱 New Crops & Farming Mechanics

### New Crops

The 1.6 update adds several new crops, each with unique mechanics:

| Crop | Season | Growth Time | Regrowth | Sell Price (Base) | Notes |
|:-----|:-------|:------------|:---------|:------------------|:------|
| **Carrot** | Spring | 3 days | No | 35g | Early-season cash crop; fast turnaround |
| **Summer Squash** | Summer | 6 days | 3 days | 45g | Regrowing crop alternative to Blueberry |
| **Broccoli** | Fall | 8 days | 4 days | 70g | High-value regrowing crop for fall |
| **Powdermelon** | Winter | 7 days | No | 60g | **Only winter crop that grows outdoors** in 1.6 |

#### Big Crops (Mega Crops)

New giant crop variants can appear in 1.6:

=== "Powdermelon Giant Crop"
    - A massive powdermelon appears on winter fields
    - Same mechanics as other giant crops (3×3 matching)
    - Drops 15–21 powdermelons when harvested with axe

=== "Qi Fruit Mega Crop"
    - Can now form giant Qi Fruit crops during the special order
    - Drops 30–50 Qi Fruit per harvest

### New Fertilizer Types

| Fertilizer | Effect | How to Get |
|:-----------|:-------|:-----------|
| **Deluxe Fertilizer** | 50% chance for iridium quality crops | Craft from 5 sap + 1 radioactive ore (Recipe from Qi) |
| **Deluxe Speed-Gro** | 35% faster growth | Can now be crafted in bulk with 1 tapper + 5 bone fragments |
| **Deluxe Retaining Soil** | Stays watered 100% in all weather | Craft from 5 stone + 1 radioactive ore |

### New Crop Behaviors

- **Fiber Seeds** now grow in **all seasons** (not just Spring 1 quest)
- **Qi Beans** grow any season, any soil
- **Winter foraging** on Meadowlands Farm no longer productive, but **Winter Seeds** can be planted and grow normally

**Source:** [Stardew Valley Wiki - Crops](https://stardewvalleywiki.com/Crops)

## 🏆 Mastery System

The Mastery system is the **most significant endgame addition** in 1.6.

### How It Works

1. Reach **level 10 in all 5 skills** to unlock Mastery
2. A **Mastery Cave** opens north of the Railroad area (once you've repaired the bridge)
3. Earn **Mastery Points** by gaining XP past level 10 (any skill)
4. Spend points on **Mastery Perks** — one per skill

### Mastery Perks

| Skill | Perk | Effect |
|:------|:-----|:-------|
| **Farming** | Golden Animal Crackers | Feed to an animal to **double its produce** permanently |
| **Mining** | Gemologist | **Gem nodes** respawn in the mine; gem prices +25% |
| **Foraging** | Mystic Tree Seeds | Plant mystic trees; wood has chance to drop **hardwood** |
| **Fishing** | Advanced Bait Maker | Craft **challenge bait** (harder fish, better rewards) and **magnetic bait** (more treasures) |
| **Combat** | Enlightenment | +1 **permanent luck**; monster drops improved; **trinket** drop rate up |

Each perk costs **10,000 Mastery Points** (first one is discounted at 5,000 for a total of 45,000 to unlock all 5).

### Mastery Rewards

Beyond perks, the Mastery system also grants:
- **Stardew Valley Almanac** — raises all skill caps to level 11
- **Anvil** — re-roll trinket stats
- **Mini-Forge** — re-forge weapon/ tool enchantments on the go

**Source:** [Stardew Valley Wiki - Mastery](https://stardewvalleywiki.com/Mastery)

## 🔧 New Machines & Crafting

### New Machines

| Machine | Recipe | Input → Output | Notes |
|:--------|:-------|:---------------|:------|
| **Dehydrator** | 30 wood + 2 fire quartz + 1 battery | Any fruit → dried fruit (sells for 2× base), any mushroom → dried mushroom (heals more) | 5 fruits at a time, takes 24h |
| **Fish Smoker** | 10 wood + 5 sap + 1 coal | Any fish → smoked fish (sells for 1.5× base quality price) | Takes 4h; stacks with Angler profession for massive profit |
| **Mushroom Log** | 10 hardwood + 10 moss | Grows mushrooms passively on your farm | Place under wild trees for variety |
| **Bait Maker** | 1 hardwood + 50 bug meat | 1 fish → 5 bait | Also produces **Wild Bait** with added fiber |
| **Heavy Tapper** | 20 wood + 1 radioactive bar | Same as tapper but **2 days faster** | Late-game upgrade; also works on mushroom trees |
| **Solar Panel** | 10 refined quartz + 5 iron + 5 gold | Generates 1 battery per sunny day | 7-day delayed start |
| **Anvil** | 20 iron bar + 1 diamond | Re-roll trinket stats | Unlocked via Mastery |
| **Mini-Forge** | 20 iron bar + 1 gold bar | Re-roll tool enchantments (anywhere) | Unlocked via Mastery |

### Key Strategy: Dehydrator vs Keg

The Dehydrator changes the fruit processing meta:

| Comparison | Keg | Dehydrator |
|:-----------|:----|:-----------|
| Process time | 6–8 days | ~24 hours |
| Multiplier | 3× (base) | 2× (base) |
| Batch size | 1 fruit | 5 fruits |
| Best use | High-value fruits (Starfruit, Ancient Fruit) | Medium-value fruits (Blueberries, Cranberries) |
| ROI per day | Lower (wait longer) | **Higher per day** for medium fruits |

For **blueberries** specifically, the Dehydrator produces dried blueberries taking only 24h vs Keg's 7 days — making it far more profitable per day for large harvests.

**Source:** [Stardew Valley Wiki - Crafting](https://stardewvalleywiki.com/Crafting)

## 🏜️ Desert Festival

The **Desert Festival** is a brand-new 3-day event replacing the old shooting gallery mini-game with a full-blown festival.

### When

- **Spring 15–17** (every year)
- Travel to the Desert via the bus

### Activities

1. **Galleon Saloon** — Play games to earn **Calico Eggs** (festival currency)
2. **Shooting Gallery** — Classic penny-pincher game, new prizes
3. **Fishing Pond** — Special desert-only fish available
4. **Minigames** — Slingshot challenge, strength game, fortune teller
5. **Peddler** — Rare items for Calico Eggs
6. **Monster Arena** — Fight waves of monsters for eggs

### Calico Egg Shop

| Item | Cost (Calico Eggs) |
|:-----|:-------------------|
| **Magic Rock Candy** | 333 |
| **Auto-Petter** | 333 |
| **Desert Trout** (fish pond) | 15 |
| **Rare Seeds** | 25–50 |
| **Desert Hat** | 12 |
| **Geode Crusher** | 50 |
| **Mummy Wrap** (clothing) | 20 |
| **Stepping Stone Path** | 1 per 10 |
| Various furniture | 5–30 |

The **Auto-Petter** being purchasable here is huge — previously only available via skull cavern treasure floors or Joja route. This gives community route players a reliable way to get one.

**Source:** [Stardew Valley Wiki - Desert Festival](https://stardewvalleywiki.com/Desert_Festival)

## 🐟 Trout Derby

A new **summer fishing festival**.

### When

- **Summer 20–21** (year 1+)

### Location

- **Cindersap Forest** (around the pond south of Marnie's)

### How It Works

- Every trout caught earns **Trout Tokens**

## 🦝 Raccoon Family

The **Raccoon** (formerly known as Trash Bear in 1.5) gets a major expansion in 1.6.

### Location

- **Southeast Cindersap Forest** (near the sewer pipe, by the Trash Bear's original spot)

### New Mechanics

After you complete the initial Trash Bear quest (clean up the surrounding area), the Raccoon **moves into a treehouse** with a family:

1. **Raccoon** — gives quests asking for specific items
2. **Mrs. Raccoon** — sells items in exchange for **moss** and **acorns**

### Raccoon Quests

- Sequential quests asking for rare items (aging barrel items, fish, crops, etc.)
- Each completion rewards **Raccoon Tokens** and unlocks more shop items
- Final reward: unlocks the ability to plant **Mushroom Tree Seeds**

### Mrs. Raccoon's Shop

| Item | Cost |
|:-----|:-----|
| **Raccoon Hat** | 5 tokens |
| **Mushroom Tree Seeds** | 10 tokens |
| **Blue Grass Starter** | 3 tokens |
| **Unique Furniture** | 5–15 tokens |
| **Moss Soup** (x2) | 2 tokens |

### Blue Grass

Blue Grass is a new grass type that:
- Grows in **blue patches** (visually distinct)
- Animals that eat blue grass produce **higher quality products**
- Can be planted via Blue Grass Starters from Mrs. Raccoon
- Spreads like normal grass

**Source:** [Stardew Valley Wiki - Raccoon](https://stardewvalleywiki.com/Raccoon)

## 👥 Multiplayer & Quality-of-Life

### Multiplayer Changes

- **Up to 8 players** on PC (was 4)
- **Shared money** option: all gold is pooled; any player can buy from any wallet
- **Split-screen** on all platforms now supports up to 4 players (was 2)
- **Building placement** by any cabin owner (not just host)
- **Farmhands can sleep anywhere** — no need to go back to cabin

### Major QoL Changes

| Change | What Changed |
|:-------|:-------------|
| **Ranged tool targeting** | Hold right-click/trigger to aim (hose, watering can, etc.) |
| **Fishing tweaks** | Bubble now shows exact fishing spot; better bite indicator |
| **Food buff bar** | Your current buffs show as **icon timer** above hotbar |
| **Chest automation** | Nearby chests in a 3×3 grid show in the crafting menu (like a crafting bench) |
| **Hay shortage warning** | Feed indicator shows on farm buildings |
| **New sorting options** | Sort by category, quality, or value in all inventories |
| **Stamina bar color** | Changes from green → yellow → red based on remaining energy |
| **Time freeze in menus** | Pausing in single-player is now instant (no 1-second delay) |
| **Redesigned Title Screen** | Farm selection shows a preview of your current save |
| **Rummageable trash** | Trash cans now give better loot in summer; chance for artifacts |
| **Easier fence repair** | Fences now last significantly longer; hardwood fences last ~5 years |
| **Book-buying prompt** | Viewing a book in inventory shows "Read?" immediately |

### New Mini-Features

- **Mushroom Boxes** (from foraging mastery) — place on farm, harvest daily mushrooms
- **Moss** — new resource from trees (20% chance per day on fully grown trees); used in new recipes
- **Fiddlehead Fern** now growable via **Fern Seeds** (crafted at foraging mastery)
- **Sap** can be turned into **fertilizer base** with new recipes
- **Fish Ponds** now produce **new items** for each fish type
- **Bombs** can be crafted with **Solar Essence** and **Void Essence**

## 🧠 Strategic Takeaways

### For New Players (Year 1)

1. **Pick Meadowlands Farm** if you want the easiest start — free chickens and hay skip 2,500g of early investments
2. **Buy carrot seeds** at Pierre's in Spring — 3-day turnaround makes them the fastest early cash crop
3. **Visit the Bookseller** every Tuesday/Wednesday/Friday — skill books accelerate your progression massively
4. **Save 5 of each fish** for the Fish Smoker — smoked fish at 1.5× price is a reliable early income stream

### For Intermediate Players (Year 2+)

1. **Unlock Mastery ASAP** after hitting level 10 across the board — the Golden Animal Cracker doubles animal income
2. **Build Dehydrators** for blueberry/cranberry harvests — faster ROI per day than kegs for medium-value crops
3. **Farm the Desert Festival** (Spring 15–17) for the Auto-Petter — community route players no longer need Joja
4. **Complete Raccoon quests** for Blue Grass Starters — higher quality animal products year-round
5. **Get the Iridium Rod** from Trout Derby (faster than waiting for Willy's shop upgrade)

### For Endgame Players

1. **Mystic Tree Seeds** (foraging mastery) + **Heavy Tappers** = passive hardwood and rare syrup income
2. **Renovate your farmhouse** — new furniture catalogs transform interior design possibilities
3. **Max out the Fish Smoker** on expensive fish (Lava Eel, Legend) — 1.5× on iridium-quality Legend = massive profit
4. **Anvil + Mini-Forge** (both from mastery) let you optimize trinkets and enchantments without returning to the forge
5. **Challenge Bait** + Seeker Hook = treasure chest farming meta
