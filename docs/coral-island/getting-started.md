# 🏝️ Coral Island — Complete Beginner Guide

## Arriving on the Island

Your adventure begins as you step off the boat into the sun-drenched town of Coral Island. The town is welcoming, the beaches are pristine, and your grandparent's overgrown farm is waiting for you. This guide walks you through **day one, your first week, your entire first season, and beyond** — so you start strong and never look back.

---

## 📅 Day 1: The Essential Start

### Morning Routine

Follow the initial NPCs through town to your farm. The tutorial is straightforward, but there are a few smart moves to make right away:

| Priority | Task | Why |
|:---------|:-----|:----|
| 1 | **Clear your farmhouse** | Place your bed and storage chest — you need a save point and inventory space |
| 2 | **Visit Sam's General Store** | Buy the cheapest seeds available (parsnips or similar quick crops) |
| 3 | **Plant and water 6-8 crops** | Don't overextend your stamina — watering 10+ with a basic can drains you |
| 4 | **Talk to at least 5 villagers** | Early relationship points compound over weeks |
| 5 | **Check your mailbox** | Tutorial quest rewards are inside |

### Stamina Management

Your energy bar is your most precious resource on day 1. Avoid chopping trees or breaking large rocks until you've planted and watered. Forage around the farm and town for **wild berries, flowers, and shells** — these can be sold for starter gold and some double as early gifts.

> **Tip:** Save 1-2 of each foraged item. You will need them for early quests and museum donations, and selling every last one is a common beginner regret.

## 💰 Week 1: Building Your Foundation

### Best Money-Making Methods

In the first week, gold is tight. Here is every viable income source ranked by effectiveness:

| Method | Estimated Daily Income | Stamina Cost | Notes |
|:-------|:----------------------|:-------------|:------|
| 🌾 Foraging | 200-400g | Low | Wild fruits, flowers, shells — free money |
| 🗑️ Trash cleanup | 150-300g | Low | Sell scrap and recyclables from the beach |
| 🎣 Fishing | 300-600g | Medium | Requires practice but reliable once you master the mini-game |
| 🤿 Diving (from Day 3) | 500-1000g | High | **Best income** once unlocked — clean trash and collect seaweed |
| ⛏️ Mining | 400-800g | High | Available from day 2, ores sell well but you will want them for upgrades |

### Diving — Your Biggest Early Advantage

Coral Island's **diving mechanic** sets it apart from other farming games. Around day 3, the diving platform near the lighthouse becomes available. Diving lets you:

- Collect **trash, seaweed, and coral** from the ocean floor
- Find **treasure chests** containing rare items and gold
- Open up **ocean pathways** to new areas
- Earn substantially more than fishing at the same stamina cost

**Recommended diving route:** start at the lighthouse dock and follow the coastal edge south. The trash respawns every 1-2 days.

### Tools to Upgrade First

| Priority | Tool | Upgrade Level | Materials | Effect |
|:---------|:-----|:-------------|:----------|:-------|
| 1 | Pickaxe | Copper | 5 Copper Ore + 1,000g | Break larger rocks, access mine depths |
| 2 | Hoe | Copper | 5 Copper Ore + 1,000g | Till larger soil patches faster |
| 3 | Watering Can | Copper | 5 Copper Ore + 1,000g | Water 3 tiles at once — huge stamina saver |
| 4 | Axe | Copper | 5 Copper Ore + 1,000g | Chop medium wood, access forest areas |

> **Tip:** Do not sell raw copper ore in the first week. You need every scrap for tool upgrades. Prioritize the pickaxe first — a copper pickaxe doubles mining speed and dramatically reduces the time you spend in the mines per day.

## 🌱 First Season: Spring

### Recommended Crops

| Crop | Seed Cost | Grow Time | Sell Price | Profit/Day |
|:-----|:----------|:----------|:-----------|:----------|
| Potato | 20g | 6 days | 60g | 6.7g/day |
| Carrot | 15g | 5 days | 45g | 6.0g/day |
| Tomato | 25g | 5 days + regrowth | 30g each | 6.0g/day (over full season) |
| Cauliflower | 50g | 10 days | 120g | 7.0g/day |

**Tomatoes are the dark horse here.** They regrow after harvest and keep producing until season end. Plant 4-6 tomato plants early and let them carry your income into the second half of spring.

### Daily Schedule Template

| Time | Activity |
|:-----|:---------|
| Morning | Water crops, check mail, process any artisan goods |
| Mid-day | Mine or dive for 3-4 in-game hours |
| Afternoon | Fish or forage until stamina runs low |
| Evening | Talk to villagers, give gifts, explore new areas |
| Night | Sell items via shipping bin, upgrade tools at blacksmith |

---

### Common Beginner Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Hurts | Better Approach |
|:--------|:-------------|:----------------|
| Overplanting first day | Runs out of stamina, misses other activities | Start with 6-8 crops, expand when tool upgrades arrive |
| Selling all ores | Delays tool upgrades by 3+ days | Only sell ores after your copper upgrades are done |
| Ignoring NPC socializing | Misses recipe gifts and quest unlocks | Talk to 3-5 villagers daily, even if no gifts |
| Forgetting fishing | Fishing is a solid consistent income | Fish for 30 minutes in-game every evening |
| Not diving | Diving is the fastest early game money | Dive every day possible in the first two weeks |

> **Final tip:** Coral Island is a marathon, not a sprint. Focus on steady expansion — add 2-3 new crop tiles each week, upgrade one tool every 5-7 days, and always keep a reserve of 500g for unexpected quests and seed restocking. By the end of Spring, you should have 15-20 crop tiles, iron tools, and a steady 2,000g/day income.
