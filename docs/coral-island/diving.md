# 🤿 Coral Island — Diving & Ocean Restoration Guide

Diving is Coral Island's signature mechanic — it replaces the traditional mining gameplay loop with underwater ocean cleaning. Unlike Stardew's mines or Mistria's caverns, diving here is about **restoration**: collecting trash, salvaging artifacts, and progressively unlocking deeper zones as you clean the ocean floor.

---

## 🔓 Unlock Conditions

| Requirement | Details |
|:------------|:--------|
| **Day** | Day 3 (after the introductory quests) |
| **Quest** | "The Ocean Needs You" — delivered via mail after Day 2 |
| **Tool** | Diving gear is provided by Ling (dock, south of farm) |
| **Cost** | Free — no upfront investment |
| **Prerequisite** | Must clear 5 trash from the shoreline (quest step) |

> **Tip:** Don't rush to Mining on Day 3. Grab your diving gear from Ling and spend Days 3–5 exclusively underwater. The early trash haul funds your seed purchases for Spring faster than any other activity.

## 🧹 Cleanup Progression Strategy

### First Pass (Days 3–15) — Profit Focus

Goal: Clear visible trash in Zone 1 and Zone 2. Sell most recyclable output immediately.

| Priority | Item | Why |
|:---------|:-----|:----|
| 1 | Rusty Gear | Highest net gain per inventory slot (+16g) |
| 2 | Battery | +13g per item |
| 3 | Machine Part | +5g per item, but rare |
| 4 | Ceramic Pot | +2g per item, good volume |
| Everything else | — | Sell or recycle — don't hoard yet |

**Expected income:** 1500–2500g per diving session (2 in-game hours)

### Second Pass (Summer+ ) — Materials Focus

Goal: Clean remaining trash, but **keep** all materials for upgrades.

| Keep? | Material | Reason |
|:------|:---------|:-------|
| ✅ Keep | Iron Scrap, Gold Scrap | Tool upgrades (watering can → iron, axe → gold) |
| ✅ Keep | Glass, Refined Glass | Sprinkler II × Sprinkler III path |
| ✅ Keep | Osmium Scrap | Rare — every piece counts for Osmium tools |
| ❌ Sell | Plastic, Rope, Cardboard | Low value, no important crafts |
| ❌ Sell | Driftwood | Actually a net loss to recycle (−1g) — sell raw instead |

## 👾 Underwater Enemies

Not all underwater creatures are friendly. These will attack you:

| Enemy | Zone | HP | Attack | Drop | Strategy |
|:------|:-----|:--:|:-------|:-----|:---------|
| 🦐 Aggressive Shrimp | Zone 1–2 | 20 | Bump damage (8 HP) | Shrimp Meat (sell 15g) | Just swim away — slower than you |
| 🐡 Pufferfish | Zone 2–3 | 40 | Poison spit (15 HP + DoT) | Toxic Spine (alchemy) | Circle-strafe — spit travels in a straight line |
| 🦑 Ink Squid | Zone 3–4 | 60 | Ink cloud (blinds 3s, 12 HP) | Ink Sac (sell 25g) | Swim upward when cloud appears — cloud sinks |
| 🦈 Reef Shark | Zone 4–5 | 150 | Bite charge (40 HP), Tail slap (25 HP) | Shark Tooth (sell 80g) | Never attack head-on. Hug walls, let it charge past, pick up loot behind it |
| 👻 Phantom Jelly | Zone 5 only | 80 | Shock tether (20 HP + stun 2s) | Void Gel (rare crafting) | Avoid entirely. Drops nothing worth fighting for |

> **Tip:** You don't need to kill anything underwater. Unlike mines, **enemies don't block your path** — the ocean is open terrain. The only reason to fight is for a specific drop. Sharks drop Shark Tooth (80g) which makes them worth farming in Zone 4 for money.

## 💨 Stamina & Oxygen Management

Diving uses a combined stamina/oxygen resource. Everything costs oxygen.

| Action | Oxygen Cost | Notes |
|:-------|:-----------:|:------|
| Swimming (empty) | 1 / sec | Base speed |
| Sprint swimming | 3 / sec | 2× speed, 3× oxygen burn |
| Collect trash | 5 per item | |
| Open chest | 10 per chest | |
| Fight (dodge) | 15 per dodge | |
| **Passive regen** | −1 / 3 sec | Only when standing still |

## 🚀 Deep Dive Tips

1. **Inventory management is critical.** Each dive yields 20–40 items. If your inventory fills, the best items (gold chests, Star Shards) decay after 30 seconds. Prioritize: Legendary artifacts > Gold chests > Gems > Metal scraps > Glass > Wood.
2. **Zone 2 is your farm spot.** Coral Garden has the highest trash density per square meter. Run Zone 2 horizontally, collect everything, surface, recycle, repeat.
3. **Ocean Rank progression path:** Bronze B (need 20 trash) → Silver B (need 100 trash) → Gold A (need 300 trash). Each rank doubles the area you can explore. Spend your early game rush on raw trash collection, not artifact hunting.
4. **Upgrade tank before fin speed.** More oxygen lets you stay down longer = more trash per session. Fin speed doesn't help if you run out of oxygen.
5. **Gold chests spawn on a timer.** Every 5 in-game hours in a fully-cleaned zone, a gold chest respawns. Farm Gold Chest routes once you reach Gold A rank.
6. **Rainy days double trash spawns.** The storm stirs up the ocean floor — new trash appears at 2× rate. Dive on every rainy day without exception.
