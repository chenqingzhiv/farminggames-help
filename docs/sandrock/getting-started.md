# 🔧 My Time at Sandrock — Workshop Starter Guide

## Setting Up Your Workshop

Your journey begins with a dilapidated workshop and a stack of debts. Your first task is to convert scrap metal and old wood into functional machinery. The tutorial walks you through building your first **Worktable** and **Stone Furnace** — these are your lifelines for the first week.

### Initial Build Order

| Priority | Item | Why |
|:---------|:-----|:----|
| 1 | Worktable | Enables basic crafting |
| 2 | Stone Furnace | Smelt copper for tool upgrades |
| 3 | Dew Collector | Passive water generation |
| 4 | Wooden Chest | Extra storage for materials |

## 💰 Earning Gols (Money)

The primary income in Sandrock comes from **commissions**. Check the Commerce Commission Board daily for available jobs. Early commissions typically ask for basic items like copper bars, wooden sticks, or simple mechanisms.

### Best Early Money Methods

| Method | Gols/Day | Risk |
|:-------|:---------|:-----|
| 📋 Town Commissions | 200-800 | Low — guaranteed payment |
| 🏚️ Ruins Salvage | 300-600 | Medium — combat required |
| 🌵 Foraging (Cactus, Sandcoral) | 100-300 | Low — free resources |
| ⛏️ Mining + Selling Excess Ore | 150-400 | Low — always profitable |

## 🛠️ Tool Upgrades

Your tools — pickaxe, axe, and hoe — can be upgraded at the **Blacksmith**. Each upgrade level increases efficiency dramatically.

| Level | Cost | Benefit |
|:------|:-----|:--------|
| Copper | 20 Copper bars | 2x gather speed |
| Iron | 30 Iron bars | 2x gather speed, can break desert rocks |
| Stainless | 50 Stainless bars | 3x gather speed + larger area |

> **Tip:** Always keep at least 5 bronze bars in your inventory for emergency commissions. Many early board requests are time-sensitive and missing a deadline hurts your reputation. If you're short on materials, the scrap piles around town respawn daily and are completely free to salvage.
