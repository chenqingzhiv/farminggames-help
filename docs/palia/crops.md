# Palia Crops Database

Gardening is one of the most rewarding and profitable skills in Palia. Whether you are a new player looking to make your first gold or a seasoned farmer optimizing your plot for maximum efficiency, understanding each crop’s grow time, seed cost, and profit per harvest is essential. This database covers every crop currently available in Palia, organized by category, with detailed tables and practical tips to help you plan your farm.

## Crop Categories Overview

Palia crops are divided into three main categories: **Vegetables**, **Fruits**, and **Special Crops**. Each category has unique growth mechanics, seed costs, and harvest yields. Vegetables are the most common and form the backbone of any farm. Fruits require more time but often yield higher profits per harvest. Special crops include rare or event-specific plants that can be used for cooking, gifting, or selling.

## Profit Analysis and Best Crops by Strategy

### Best Crops for Early Game (First 20 Hours)

If you are just starting out, focus on crops with low seed costs and fast grow times. Carrots and Potatoes are your best friends. Plant as many as your energy allows, and reinvest your profits into more seeds.

- **Carrot:** 6-hour grow time, 20 gold profit per harvest. You can plant and harvest 4 times in a single day.
- **Potato:** 8-hour grow time, 35 gold profit per harvest. Slightly better profit per hour than carrots.

### Best Crops for Mid Game (20-50 Hours)

Once you have a steady gold flow, switch to higher-profit crops like Tomatoes, Corn, and Blueberries. These crops offer better returns per garden plot.

- **Tomato:** 12-hour grow time, 65 gold profit per harvest. Great for cooking and selling.
- **Blueberry:** 24-hour grow time, 150 gold profit per harvest. Perfect for daily harvest cycles.

### Best Crops for Late Game (50+ Hours)

Late-game players should focus on Grapes, Starfruit, and Mushrooms. These crops require patience but yield the highest profits and are essential for end-game recipes.

- **Grape:** 48-hour grow time, 300 gold profit per harvest. Best profit per plot.
- **Starfruit:** 72-hour grow time, 350 gold profit per harvest. Ideal for special dishes.

## Gardening Mechanics and Tips

### Watering and Fertilizer

All crops require regular watering. You can use a watering can or build sprinklers to automate the process. Fertilizer can be applied to reduce grow time or increase yield. Quality fertilizer reduces grow time by 20%, while Rich Fertilizer increases harvest yield by 1 unit.

### Crop Rotation and Soil Quality

Soil quality degrades over time. Planting the same crop repeatedly reduces yield. Rotate your crops to maintain soil health. Using fertilizer also helps replenish soil nutrients.

### Multi-Harvest Crops

Some crops, like Tomatoes and Grapes, can be harvested multiple times. After the initial harvest, the plant will regrow in half the original grow time. This makes them extremely efficient for long-term farming.

## Quick Tips

- **Start small:** Focus on Carrots and Potatoes until you have at least 500 gold.
- **Automate early:** Build sprinklers as soon as possible to save time and energy.
- **Use fertilizer:** Quality Fertilizer on high-value crops like Grapes can significantly boost your daily profit.
- **Sell raw or cook:** Cooking ingredients into meals often doubles or triples their value. Check recipes before selling.
- **Gift wisely:** Some villagers love specific crops. Gifting a Starfruit to a villager can increase friendship faster than any other gift.
- **Plan your layout:** Leave space for trellises if you plan to grow fruits like Grapes. A well-organized garden saves time and maximizes space.
- **Check the market:** Crop prices fluctuate daily. Sell when prices are high for maximum profit.
