# Palia Quick Tips for Beginners

New to Kilima Valley? These six tips will save you time, gold, and frustration as you settle into Palia.

## 1. Keep Everything (At First)

It's tempting to sell items for quick gold, but nearly every resource has a use — whether for crafting, gifting, or completing quest bundles. Stash everything in your storage chest and only sell when you genuinely need the cash.

## 2. Talk to Everyone Daily

Each villager resets their daily conversation and gift limit, and chatting costs nothing. Building friendship unlocks quests, recipes, and even free tools, so make the rounds whenever you log in.

## 3. Upgrade Your Axe and Pick First

Better tools mine rarer ores and chop larger trees, which gates nearly every crafting progression. Prioritize copper upgrades for your axe and pick before spending resources on a better hoe or watering can.

## 4. Join a Community

Palia is kinder with friends. Calling out rare resources like flow trees or palium in chat lets everyone strike it for shared loot — and asking for help with cooking or hunting makes those activities far more efficient.

## 5. Watch Your Focus

Focus acts as an XP multiplier for every skill action. Cook meals at your campfire and eat before gathering, mining, or hunting to level up noticeably faster. Renown can be spent at the Phoenix Shrine to increase your focus pool.

## 6. Unlock Fast Travel Early

The stable board in town sells fast-travel points for a modest gold fee. Unlocking the major zones early saves you endless running and makes daily errand loops much more pleasant.
