## How Fishing Works in Palia

Fishing in Palia is a core skill that rewards you with food ingredients, quest items, and valuable resources. Each body of water has its own unique fish pool. Your success depends on three main factors:

- **Location**: Rivers, lakes, ponds, and coastal waters each host different species.
- **Bait**: Standard worms work for most fish, but glow worms and special bait are required for rare and epic catches.
- **Time of Day**: Some fish only appear during the day (6:00–18:00), others at night (18:00–6:00), and a few are active 24/7.

You can also use fishing hooks and buffs from food to increase your catch rate, but the tables below assume no modifiers.

## Bundle Information

Bundles are a major endgame goal in Palia. Completing them rewards you with unique furniture, recipes, and progression items. Here’s exactly which fish you need for each bundle.

### Fisherman’s Bundle

- Catfish (Kilima River, Night, Worm)
- Longnose Gar (Bahari River, Night, Glow Worm)
- 1 additional uncommon fish of your choice

**Reward**: Fishing Rod Stand (decorative item)

### Oceanic Bundle

- Giant Stingray (Kilima Coast, Any, Glow Worm)
- Coral Trout (Bahari Coast, Day, Glow Worm)
- 1 additional ocean fish of uncommon or higher rarity

**Reward**: Seashell Chime (wall decoration)

### Night Sky Bundle

- Void Ray (Kilima River, Night, Glow Worm)
- Midnight Pike (Bahari River, Night, Glow Worm)
- Glowing Koi (Underground Lake, Night, Glow Worm)

**Reward**: Starry Night Lantern (light source)

### Master Angler Bundle

- Kilima Sturgeon (Kilima River, Any, Glow Worm)
- Ancient Fish (Kilima Coast, Night, Glow Worm)
- Mythic Fish (Bahari Coast, Night, Glow Worm)
- Crystal Carp (Underground Lake, Any, Glow Worm)

**Reward**: Master Angler Trophy (display item) + 5,000 Gold

## Bait Guide

Knowing which bait to use saves you time and resources.

- **Worm Bait**: Standard bait. Catches all common and uncommon fish. Can be dug from the ground or purchased from the General Store for 10 Gold each.
- **Glow Worm Bait**: Required for all rare and epic fish. Crafted using 1 Worm Bait + 1 Brightshroom. Brightshrooms are found in caves and near water at night.
- **Special Bait**: Some limited-time events introduce unique bait, but glow worms are your primary tool for high-tier fishing.

**Pro Tip**: Always carry at least 50 glow worms when heading to Bahari Bay or the Underground. Rare fish appear more frequently in these zones, and you don’t want to waste a trip.

## Quick Tips

1. **Always fish at night for epics.** Every epic fish in Palia (except Crystal Carp) is only available between 18:00 and 6:00. Plan your fishing trips accordingly.
2. **Use glow worms for bundles.** If you’re working on bundles, skip worm bait entirely. Glow worms are the only way to catch the rare and epic fish required.
3. **Fish in the Underground for variety.** The Underground Lake is the only place to catch Glowing Koi and Crystal Carp, both needed for bundles. It’s also a great spot for leveling up.
4. **Don’t sell rare fish.** Before selling any fish, check if it’s needed for a bundle. You’ll save yourself hours of frustration later.
5. **Eat fishing buff food.** Dishes like Fisherman’s Brew (+2 Fishing) and Spicy Fish Stew (+3 Fishing) increase your catch rate and reduce the time fish take to bite. Always cook these before a serious fishing session.
6. **Upgrade your rod first.** Spend your early gold on the Standard Rod, then the Fine Rod. The longer cast and faster reel speed make a huge difference when chasing rare fish.

Happy fishing, and may your line always be tight! For more Palia guides, check out our crop database and crafting recipes.
