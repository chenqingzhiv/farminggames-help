# Palia — Crop Profit Guide

Optimize your garden layout and crop selection in Palia with this complete profit guide. Unlike most farming games, Palia uses a garden-plot grid system where crops can be planted individually or in rows, and quality (star value) affects sell prices.

> **Note:** All prices shown are base (non-star) quality. Star-quality crops sell for roughly 1.5× base price. Gardening skill level and fertilizer can also boost yield and quality.

## Star Quality Value Multiplier

| Quality | Price Multiplier | Example: Carrot |
|:---|---:|---:|
| ⭐ Base (No Star) | 1.0× | 18 Gold |
| ⭐⭐ Star Quality | ~1.5× | 27 Gold |
| ⭐⭐⭐ Rare Star | ~2.0× | 36 Gold |

Star quality is random but can be increased with:
- **Quality Up Fertilizer** — Boosts star quality chance by 25%
- **Gardening Skill Level** — Each level slightly increases base quality chance
- **Special Soil Plots** — Some home plot layouts have natural quality bonuses

## Gardening Tips

- **Watering:** Crops only grow when watered. Unwatered crops pause growth but don't die.
- **Weeds:** Weeds spawn randomly on unplanted soil plots. Remove them before they spread.
- **Fertilizer:** Quality Up Fertilizer is best for high-base-price crops (Ginger, Cabbage, Mushroom).
- **Multi-harvest:** Tomato, Corn, Cotton, Pepper, and Blueberry produce multiple items per harvest — great for cooking ingredients.
- **Gifting:** Star-quality Blueberries are a liked gift for many NPCs; Star-quality Sweet Leaf is a weekly want for some.
- **Plot expansion:** Invest in extra soil plots as soon as you level up — more plots = more gold.
