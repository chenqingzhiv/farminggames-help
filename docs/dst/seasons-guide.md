# 🌤️ Don't Starve Together — Complete Seasons & Survival Guide

> *Master every season in DST — from the gentle days of Autumn to the scorching hazards of Summer. Know what's coming, prepare for it, and thrive.*

Don't Starve Together's seasons are the heartbeat of the game. Each one lasts roughly **20 days** (configurable in world settings), and a full year cycle is **70 days** by default. Each season brings unique weather, hazards, creatures, and opportunities. Your survival depends on anticipating what's next and preparing before it arrives.

<small style="opacity: 0.6;">Data source: Klei Entertainment / Don't Starve Together game files (various patches). Values accurate as of current stable release.</small>

## 📅 Season Cycle Overview

| Season | Days | Temperature | Day/Night Balance | Key Hazard | Key Boss |
|:-------|:-----|:------------|:------------------|:-----------|:---------|
| 🍂 **Autumn** | 1–20 | 10–40°C (comfortable) | ~12 min day, ~6 min dusk, ~2 min night | None | Bearger (days 20–25) |
| ❄️ **Winter** | 21–35 | Below 0°C (freezing) | ~7 min day, ~5 min dusk, ~8 min night | Freezing | Deerclops (days 30–35) |
| 🌧️ **Spring** | 35–50 | 10–25°C (wet) | ~10 min day, ~5 min dusk, ~5 min night | Rain, Wetness, Lightning | Moose/Goose (throughout Spring) |
| ☀️ **Summer** | 50–70 | 35–80°C (overheating) | ~16 min day, ~4 min dusk, ~0 min night | Overheating, Wildfires | Antlion (days 55–70), Dragonfly (lava biome) |

> **World settings note:** All season lengths can be customized in the world generation menu. "Short" seasons last ~15 days, "Long" seasons ~40 days. The default 20-day length is used throughout this guide.

## 🍂 Autumn (Days 1–20)

Autumn is the **starter season** — the most forgiving and productive period in DST. You spawn in on Day 1 of Autumn with comfortable temperatures, long daylight hours, and no pressing environmental threats.

### What to Expect

| Aspect | Detail |
|:-------|:-------|
| 🌡️ **Temperature** | 10–40°C — perfectly comfortable, no heat or cold damage |
| ☀️ **Day/Night** | ~12 min day, ~6 min dusk, ~2 min night — plenty of daylight |
| 🌧️ **Precipitation** | Very rare, short rain showers |
| ⚠️ **Hazards** | None (spiders, hounds, and frogs are your only concerns) |

### 🎯 Key Activities

Autumn is your **preparation window**. Every hour spent gathering now pays off tenfold in Winter.

#### Gather Everything
| Resource | Why It Matters |
|:---------|:---------------|
| 🪵 **Logs** | Fuel, structures, base building — you can never have too many |
| 🪨 **Rocks, Flint, Gold** | Tools, weapons, SCIENCE MACHINE (1× gold, 4× rocks) |
| 🌿 **Grass & Twigs** | Rope, torches, basic crafting — stockpile at least 2 stacks each |
| 🫐 **Berries** | Food now and rot for fertilizer later |
| 🌾 **Cut Reeds** | For papyrus (books, bird traps) from swamp biomes |

#### Build Your Starter Base

1. **Choose a location** — Ideally near a road intersection with access to multiple biomes. The Pig King biome is a popular choice.
2. **Science Machine** — Your first build. Unlocks alchemy engine, backpack, hammer, and more.
3. **Alchemy Engine** — Unlocks the rest of the tech tree.
4. **Crock Pot** (×2 minimum) — The single best food-related structure. Cooks meals that restore more hunger, health, and sanity than raw ingredients.
5. **Ice Box** — Preserves food 4× longer. Essential for stockpiling.
6. **Drying Racks** (×4–8) — Dry monster meat, fish, and morsels into jerky for hunger + sanity.
7. **Farm plots** — If you plan to garden, lay out plots now so crops mature before Winter.
8. **Campfire/Pit** — Upgrade to a Fire Pit as soon as possible (uses less fuel).

### 🐻 Bearger (Spawn: Days 20–25)

The Bearger arrives at the **end of Autumn or early Winter**. Its spawn is triggered automatically — it will wander into your general area.

| Stat | Value |
|:-----|:------|
| ❤️ **HP** | 3000 |
| ⚔️ **Damage** | 100 (to players), 200 (to structures) |
| 😵 **Sanity Aura** | -400/min (increasing fear near it) |
| 🎁 **Drops** | 6× Meat, 2× Bearger Fur, Bearger Figure (Sketch) |

#### Kiting Strategy
- **Pattern:** Attack 6 times, then dodge its swipe
- **Weapon:** Hambat (or better)
- **Armour:** Log Suit + Football Helmet
- **Tip:** Bearger has a huge hitbox and can be stunlocked briefly — use this to get extra hits

#### Alternative: Let Treeguards Handle It
Lead the Bearger into a dense pine forest. It will destroy trees, spawning **Treeguards** that fight it. You can clean up the winner.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Bearger destroys structures. Do NOT fight it near your base. Lead it to a clearing or pre-built fighting arena.

### ✅ Winter Preparation Checklist

Use the last 5 days of Autumn to tick off these items:

| Item | Quantity | Notes |
|:-----|:---------|:------|
| 🧣 **Thermal Stone** | 1–2 | Heat retention — place near fire to warm, carry to stay warm |
| 🎩 **Winter Hat / Beefalo Hat** | 1 | Beefalo Hat is best (240s of warmth) |
| 🥩 **Dried Meat / Jerky** | 10+ | Long shelf life, good hunger/sanity |
| 🥘 **Pre-cooked Meals** | 10+ | Meatballs, Bacon & Eggs, Meaty Stew |
| 🪵 **Logs** | 5+ stacks | Constant fuel for fires |
| 🩹 **Healing Salves / Honey Poultices** | 5+ | For combat healing |
| 🧥 **Log Suit + Football Helmet** | 1 each | For Deerclops fight |
| ❄️ **Ice stockpile location** | — | Mark a pond biome on your map for mining ice |

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Kill some **Beefalo** for their horns (to craft the Beefalo Hat). Do this before they enter mating season (Winter) when they become aggressive.

## ❄️ Winter (Days 21–35)

Winter is the **first real survival challenge**. Freezing temperatures drain your health if you're not properly equipped. Hunger drains faster. Ponds freeze. Berry bushes go dormant. The nights stretch long.

### What to Expect

| Aspect | Detail |
|:-------|:-------|
| 🌡️ **Temperature** | Below 0°C — freezing damage without warm clothing |
| ☀️ **Day/Night** | ~7 min day, ~5 min dusk, ~8 min night — short days |
| 🌧️ **Precipitation** | Snow, no rain |
| ⚠️ **Hazards** | Freezing (constant health drain), starvation, shorter foraging time |

### ❄️ Temperature & Warmth Mechanics

Your character has a **core temperature** that drops in Winter. When it drops too low:

- **Screen frosts** around the edges
- **Health drains** at an increasing rate
- **Movement speed** slows

#### Staying Warm — Clothing Comparison

| Item | Warmth Duration | Materials | Notes |
|:-----|:----------------|:----------|:------|
| 🎩 **Beefalo Hat** | 240s | Beefalo Horn ×1, Silk ×4 | Best warmth-to-slot ratio |
| 🧥 **Puffy Vest** | 180s | Koalefant Trunk ×4, Silk ×6 | Chest slot, good insulation |
| 🧥 **Hibearnation Vest** | 180s | Bearger Fur ×2, Silk ×4 | Chest slot, also slows hunger |
| 🎩 **Winter Hat** | 120s | Silk ×4, Hound's Tooth ×2 | Easy to craft, decent starter |
| 🐇 **Cat Cap** | 60s | Catcoon Tail ×4, Silk ×2 | Weak — emergency only |
| 🧣 **Thermal Stone** | ~150s per heat | Stone ×10, Flint ×3, Pickaxe | Heat near fire, carry to stay warm |

#### Thermal Stone Mastery

The **Thermal Stone** is your most efficient warmth tool:

1. **Heat it up** — Place near a fire until it's red-hot (glowing)
2. **Carry it** — The stone radiates heat for ~150 seconds in Winter
3. **Reheat** — Drop near fire when the glow fades
4. **Stacking** — Carry two stones; heat one while using the other

> **⚠️ **Warning:** Thermal Stones lose durability in Summer (overheat) if updated to the feature. In current DST, they don't break but cool/heat differently.

### 🍲 Winter Food

Food is tighter in Winter. Your best options:

| Food Source | Method | Notes |
|:------------|:-------|:------|
| ❄️ **Ice** | Mine with pickaxe from frozen ponds | Abundant, lasts forever, use in Crock Pot |
| 🐧 **Pengulls** | Near coastline | 2 meat each, aggressive in groups |
| 🐗 **Pig Farm** | Feed pigs monster meat, kill the werepigs | 2 meat per pig |
| 🦌 **MacTusk** (Walrus Camp) | Hunt every 2.5 days | Drops Tam o' Shanter + Walking Cane |
| 🐟 **Fishing** | Not possible (ponds frozen) | — |

#### Ice Is Your Best Friend
Ice is a **free filler ingredient** for the Crock Pot. Learn these recipes:

| Recipe | Ingredients | Effect |
|:-------|:------------|:-------|
| 🥣 **Meatballs** | 1× Meat + 3× Ice | 62.5 Hunger — your staple food |
| 🥟 **Meat Dumplings** | 1× Meat + 1× Egg + 1× Veg + 1× Ice | 37.5 Hunger + 40 Health — heals |
| 🥗 **Honey Nuggets** | 1× Honey + 1× Morsel + 2× Ice | 37.5 Hunger + 20 Health |

### 🦷 MacTusk & Walrus Camps

Walrus Camps spawn in **Rockyland biomes** during Winter. They're your most valuable resource.

| Detail | Info |
|:-------|:-----|
| **Spawn** | 1 Walrus Camp per world (default) |
| **Respawn** | Every 2.5 days — MacTusk + 2 Hounds |
| **MacTusk HP** | 100 |
| **Hound HP** | 100 each |

**🎁 MacTusk Drops:**
- 🎩 **Tam o' Shanter** (25%) — 6.25 Sanity/min, best sanity head slot
- 🦯 **Walking Cane** (25%) — 25% movement speed boost (off-hand)
- 🥩 **Meat** (always)

#### Farming MacTusk
1. Approach from **downwind** (MacTusk prioritises running away)
2. Kill the **Hounds first** (they chase, MacTusk shoots)
3. Chase down MacTusk — it has low HP and dies quickly
4. Bring a **Boomerang** if it runs onto thin ice

### 🦌 Deerclops (Spawn: Days 30–35)

Deerclops arrives in **late Winter** — a massive, one-eyed giant that freezes everything around it.

| Stat | Value |
|:-----|:------|
| ❤️ **HP** | 4000 |
| ⚔️ **Damage** | 75 (to players), 150 (to structures) |
| 😵 **Sanity Aura** | -400/min |
| ❄️ **Freezing Attack** | Freezes everything in a cone (area of effect) |
| 🎁 **Drops** | 8× Meat, Deerclops Eyeball (crafts Eyebrella) |

#### Kiting Strategy
- **Pattern:** Attack 2–3 times, then dodge the ground slam
- **Weapon:** Hambat (or Dark Sword for extra damage)
- **Armour:** Log Suit + Football Helmet (bring spares)
- **Space:** Fight in a large open area — Deerclops destroys structures and trees

#### Eyebrella — The Key to Spring
The **Deerclops Eyeball** crafts the **Eyebrella** (at an Alchemy Engine):

| Material | Quantity |
|:---------|:---------|
| Deerclops Eyeball | 1 |
| Bone Shards | 6 |
| Dubloon | 4 |

The Eyebrella provides **100% wetness resistance** — it's the single best item for Spring survival.

> **💡 Pro Tip:** If you're not confident fighting Deerclops, lead it to a swamp biome. Tentacles will kill it for you. Just make sure you collect the eyeball before it despawns!

## 🌧️ Spring (Days 35–50)

Spring is the **rain season**. Constant downpours drench your character, draining sanity and making everything harder. Lightning strikes randomly. Frogs fall from the sky. But it's also a season of abundance.

### What to Expect

| Aspect | Detail |
|:-------|:-------|
| 🌡️ **Temperature** | 10–25°C — comfortable temp, but wet |
| ☀️ **Day/Night** | ~10 min day, ~5 min dusk, ~5 min night — balanced |
| 🌧️ **Precipitation** | Constant rain, occasional thunderstorms |
| ⚠️ **Hazards** | Wetness (sanity loss, freezing vulnerability), lightning, Frog Rain |

### 💧 Wetness Mechanics

When you get wet:

| Wetness % | Effect |
|:----------|:-------|
| 0–15% | No penalty |
| 15–35% | Sanity drain begins (slow) |
| 35–70% | Faster sanity drain, movement penalty |
| 70–100% | Maximum sanity drain, freezing vulnerability, tools slip |

#### Staying Dry — Rain Protection

| Item | Wetness Resist | Materials | Notes |
|:-----|:---------------|:----------|:------|
| 🪶 **Eyebrella** | 100% | Deerclops Eyeball + Bone Shards + Dubloon | Perfect — best in slot (repair with Deerclops Eyeball or Deconstruction Staff) |
| 🌂 **Umbrella** | 66% | Silk ×6, Twigs ×4 | Easy to craft, decent coverage |
| 🎩 **Rain Hat** | 90% | Catcoon Tail ×2, Silk ×4 | Good but materials can be annoying |
| 🧥 **Rain Coat** | 100% | Tentacle Spots ×4, Bone Shards ×4, Silk ×4 | Full coverage, chest slot |
| 🪣 **Rainometer/Umbrella Hat** | — | Crafted | Not wearable — it's a structure that measures rainfall |

> **💡 Pro Tip:** You can combine a Football Helmet with an Umbrella (hold in off-hand) for ~66% wetness resist + head protection. Stack this with a Puffy Vest for the best combat setup in Spring.

### ⚡ Lightning & WX-78

Spring thunderstorms bring lightning strikes that:
- 💥 **Damage players** (30 damage) unless wearing a lightning rod hat
- 🔥 **Ignite flammable structures** — always build **Lightning Rods** near your base
- ⚡ **Power up WX-78** — WX-78 gains +100 max HP, +200 max sanity per overcharge (but still takes damage)

**Protection:** Build 2–3 Lightning Rods around your base. They protect a generous radius and only need to be crafted once.

### 🐸 Frog Rain (Days 40–50)

Around mid-Spring, frogs begin raining from the sky. This is both a blessing and a curse.

- **Frogs** can spawn in massive numbers — up to 20+ at once
- They **steal items** from your inventory when they hit you
- They attack in **groups**

#### Frog Rain Strategy

| Approach | How To | Risk/Benefit |
|:---------|:-------|:-------------|
| 🏠 **Stay inside** | Seal yourself in your base | Safe but boring |
| 🐗 **Use pigs** | Lead frogs to pig villages | Pigs fight frogs — free frog legs |
| 🦆 **Run away** | Leave the loaded area | Frogs despawn eventually |
| ⚔️ **Harvest them** | Fight with a spear, collect legs | Massive food (each frog = 1 frog leg) |

**Reward:** Each frog drops a **Frog Leg** (cooks into 12.5 hunger, 5 health). A good frog rain can yield 2+ stacks — that's unlimited food for the rest of Spring.

### 🦆 Moose/Goose & Moslings

The **Moose/Goose** is a giant bird that appears in Spring, nesting in grassy areas.

| Stat | Value |
|:-----|:------|
| ❤️ **HP** | 3000 |
| ⚔️ **Damage** | 75 (melee), 200 (honk blast) |
| 🎁 **Drops** | 4× Drumstick, 2× Feather, Moose/Goose Figure (Sketch) |

#### Fighting Moose/Goose
- **Kiting:** Attack 4–5 times, dodge the honk (ground-pounding AOE)
- **Weakness:** It's slow and telegraphs attacks
- **Danger:** The honk blast hits in a huge radius — back away when it rears up

#### Moslings (Eggs)
The Moose/Goose nest contains 2–3 **Mosling Eggs**:
- **Hatch** in ~3 days if kept warm (near a fire)
- **Moslings follow you** and fight for you
- **Grow into** Moose/Goose after ~12 days (if not killed)
- **Use:** Excellent early-game bodyguard. Moslings deal ~25 damage per hit.

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Pick up Mosling Eggs and keep them near a campfire to hatch. The Mosling will follow and fight for you — it's like having a free tank for exploring the ruins!

### 🌻 Spring Activities

| Activity | Why It's Great in Spring |
|:---------|:------------------------|
| 🐝 **Bee Farming** | Flowers bloom everywhere — bees produce constantly |
| 🌱 **Farming** | Rain waters crops automatically — best season for farming |
| 🕳️ **Cave Exploration** | No freeze, no overheat — comfortable underground |
| 🔮 **Magic Crafting** | Longer dusk gives more shadow fuel collection time |

## ☀️ Summer (Days 50–70)

Summer is the **second major survival challenge**. Overheating burns your health. Wildfires destroy your base. Days are brutally long. You'll stay alive only if you prepared in the previous seasons.

### What to Expect

| Aspect | Detail |
|:-------|:-------|
| 🌡️ **Temperature** | 35–80°C — overheating damage without cooling |
| ☀️ **Day/Night** | ~16 min day, ~4 min dusk, ~0 min night — almost no darkness |
| 🌧️ **Precipitation** | None — bone dry |
| ⚠️ **Hazards** | Overheating (health drain), Wildfires (smoldering), Antlion sinkholes |

### 🔥 Overheating Mechanics

When your core temperature gets too high:

- **Screen shimmers** with heat waves
- **Health drains** at an increasing rate
- **Food spoils faster**
- **Ice melts** in your inventory

#### Cooling Down — Methods

| Method | Cooling Effect | Notes |
|:-------|:---------------|:------|
| 🔵 **Endothermic Fire Pit** | Strong cooling (area) | Build with ice + logs — use near base |
| 🔵 **Endothermic Campfire** | Moderate cooling (area) | Portable, same recipe, lasts less time |
| 🧊 **Chilled Thermal Stone** | ~150s cooling | Place thermal stone near Endothermic Fire or drop ice on it |
| 💎 **Chilled Amulet** | Constant cooling | Craft at Shadow Manipulator (1× Red Gem, 3× Nightmare Fuel, 2× Gold) |
| ❄️ **Ice Cube Hat** | Head slot cooling | Melts over time, refreeze in fridge |

#### Endothermic Fire Recipe
```
1× Ice + 2× Logs → Endothermic Fire Pit
```

#### Thermal Stone Cooling
1. **Heat your Thermal Stone** near a normal fire
2. **Drop it on the ground** near ice or in a fridge briefly
3. **Wait** — it turns from red → white → blue (cooled)
4. **Carry the blue stone** — it absorbs ambient heat and keeps you ~20°C cooler

> **⚠️ Warning:** A red-hot Thermal Stone carried into Summer will overheat you faster. Always cool it down first at an Endothermic Fire.

### 🔥 Wildfires (Smoldering)

Starting in Summer, flammable structures and items can **spontaneously smolder** and catch fire.

| Flammable | Risk Level |
|:----------|:-----------|
| 🪵 **Trees** | High — especially dry/diseased trees |
| 🌿 **Grass Tufts / Saplings** | Medium |
| 🧱 **Wood Walls / Floors** | High |
| 🗿 **Stone structures** | None (mostly fireproof) |
| 🪨 **Rock Walls** | None |

#### Smoldering Stages
1. **Smoke** appears above the item — you have ~10 seconds to act
2. **Fire** erupts — spreads to nearby flammable objects
3. **Destruction** — the item burns to ash

#### Stopping Wildfires
| Method | How To | Notes |
|:-------|:-------|:------|
| 🧊 **Ice Flingomatic** | Automatically extinguishes fires in range | Must be fueled (ice, snowballs) — 5-tile radius |
| ⛲ **Water Balloon** | Throw to douse single item | Craft with water + berries |
| 🪓 **Chop it down** | Remove smoldering trees/grass before they ignite | Rush job, might still catch fire |
| 💧 **Ice on ground** | Drop ice on smoldering items | Consumes ice quickly (not recommended for large fires) |

#### Ice Flingomatic Setup
1. **Craft** at Alchemy Engine: (2× Gears, 2× Ice, 1× Electrical Doodad)
2. **Place** covering your entire base (a circle ~5 tiles radius)
3. **Fuel** with ice or snowballs — each ice lasts ~2.5 days
4. **Toggle ON** — you can turn it off when away from base (to save fuel)

Build **2–3 Ice Flingomatics** to cover a large base completely.

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Stone and marble structures don't burn. Prioritise stone walls and foundations near critical structures if you can't cover everything with Flingomatics.

### 👑 Antlion (Spawn: Days 55–70)

The Antlion appears in the **Desert biome** during Summer. Unlike other bosses, you can **appease** it instead of fighting.

| Stat | Value |
|:-----|:------|
| ❤️ **HP** | 3000 |
| ⚔️ **Damage** | 75 (melee), 150 (sand spike) |
| ⚠️ **Spawn Effect** | Causes sinkholes and desert earthquakes if angered |

#### Sinkhole Mechanics
If you don't appease the Antlion, it triggers **sinkholes** near your base:
- Sinkholes destroy structures
- They open randomly in loaded areas
- Frequency increases the longer you ignore it

#### Appeasing the Antlion
Give the Antlion one of these items at its sinkhole:

| Item | Effect | Availability |
|:-----|:--------|:-------------|
| 🎪 **Trinkets** | 1 trinket = ~4 days of peace | Found in tumbleweeds, graves |
| ⚙️ **Spare Gears** | 1 gear = ~6 days | Clockwork enemies in ruins |
| 🍲 **Any Food** | 4 food = 1 day | Weak — not worth it after early game |

#### Fighting Antlion (If You Prefer)
1. **Lure** it out of its sinkhole with a ranged attack
2. **Kiting:** Attack 3 times, dodge sand spikes (they telegraph)
3. **Avoid:** The ground-shaking AOE — move away when it rears up
4. **Warning:** Antlion's spikes are faster than they look

**Drops:** Desert Stone Golem (mount item), 4× Meat, Antlion Figure (Sketch)

### 🐉 Dragonfly (Lava Biome Boss)

Dragonfly is technically accessible year-round but **most often fought in Summer** because the extreme heat is already part of the environment.

| Stat | Value |
|:-----|:------|
| ❤️ **HP** | 27,500 |
| ⚔️ **Damage** | 75 (melee), 300 (fire blast) |
| 🎁 **Drops** | 8× Meat, Dragonfly Scales (crafts Scalemail + Fireproof armour) |

> **📝 Note:** Dragonfly is more of a dedicated boss fight than a seasonal event. See the [Bosses & Giants](/dst/bosses/) page for detailed strategies.

### 🏠 Base Location Tips for Summer

| Strategy | How To | Effectiveness |
|:---------|:-------|:--------------|
| 🏝️ **Oasis biome** | Build in the Desert Oasis | Pond keeps area cool, Antlion is nearby for appeasing |
| 🕳️ **Underground base** | Move operations to caves | No overheating at all — but no rain and no surface resources |
| 🌲 **Forest shade** | Build among dense trees | Trees provide some shade cooling (minor) |
| 🪨 **Rockyland** | Build with stone foundations | Stone doesn't burn — safer from wildfires |
| 🏖️ **Near water** | Coast/pond proximity | Water bodies radiate minor cooling (very minor) |

> **💡 Pro Tip for Beginners:** The **Oasis Desert** is the safest Summer base location. The lake keeps the area cooler, tumbleweeds provide infinite trinkets for Antlion appeasement, and the Antlion itself is right there to manage.

## 💡 Pro Tips for Each Season

### 🍂 Autumn Pro Tips
1. **Kill Koalefant ASAP** — The trunk crafts the Puffy Vest, best-insulation chest slot for Winter. Track its footprints early.
2. **Mark ice ponds on your map** — You'll need to know where to mine ice in Winter. Visit every pond biome and drop a marker.
3. **Make a Bird Trap** — Catch a crow, build a bird cage. Feed it cooked monster meat for eggs. Eggs never spoil and are amazing Crock Pot filler.
4. **Kill spiders for silk** — You need 14 silk for a Top Hat (sanity) + all the drying racks and winter clothes. Clear spider dens early.

### ❄️ Winter Pro Tips
1. **Double-carry Thermal Stones** — Keep one in the fire and one on you. When yours cools, swap and reheat the cold one.
2. **MacTusk is THE priority** — The Tam o' Shanter and Walking Cane are game-changing. Hunt Walrus Camps on cooldown (every 2.5 days).
3. **Don't explore too far** — Short days mean long nights. Stay near your base or bring enough light sources for the full night.
4. **Ice mining marathon** — On the last day of Winter, mine ALL the ice you can carry. Store it in fridges for Summer cooling and year-round Crock Pot filler.

### 🌧️ Spring Pro Tips
1. **Eyebrella > everything** — If you killed Deerclops, the Eyebrella is your #1 Spring item. 100% wetness resist means no sanity loss from rain. Repair it with Deconstruction Staff or another Eyeball.
2. **Prepare bee boxes now** — Spring flowers bloom everywhere. Set up 4–6 bee boxes for year-round honey (infinite Crock Pot filler + healing).
3. **Frog rain = unlimited food** — Don't hide from it. Gear up and fight in an open field. Collect 30+ frog legs by kiting them one by one.
4. **Explore caves** — Spring is the best time to descend into caves. Surface is wet but caves are dry and comfortable.

### ☀️ Summer Pro Tips
1. **Ice Flingomatic placement matters** — Space them so their 5-tile radii overlap at your base center. Use the geometric placement mod or measure with turf tiles.
2. **Oasis base is best** — If you can, move a secondary camp to the Oasis Desert for Summer. It's cooler, has tumbleweed loot, and Antlion is close by.
3. **Don't forget about food** — Summer heat spoils food faster. Crock Pot meals spoil slower than raw ingredients. Keep a few extra Ice Boxes.
4. **Caves are 100% safe** — If you're struggling with Summer, just move underground for 20 days. No overheating, no wildfires, no Antlion sinkholes. The only threats are cave-specific (depth worms, bunnymen if you carry meat).
5. **Summer is for ruins** — The Labyrinth and Ruins have a constant temperature. Bring a Miner Hat, lanterns, and go clear them for Thulecite and Ancient gear.
