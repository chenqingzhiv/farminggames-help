    Palia Crops Database

    Gardening is one of the most rewarding and profitable skills in Palia. Whether you are a new player looking to make your first gold or a seasoned farmer optimizing your plot for maximum efficiency, understanding each crop’s grow time, seed cost, and profit per harvest is essential. This database covers every crop currently available in Palia, organized by category, with detailed tables and practical tips to help you plan your farm.

    Crop Categories Overview

    Palia crops are divided into three main categories: Vegetables, Fruits, and Special Crops. Each category has unique growth mechanics, seed costs, and harvest yields. Vegetables are the most common and form the backbone of any farm. Fruits require more time but often yield higher profits per harvest. Special crops include rare or event-specific plants that can be used for cooking, gifting, or selling.

    Vegetable Crops

    Vegetables are the foundation of Palia gardening. They grow quickly, have lower seed costs, and are excellent for generating consistent income. Below is the complete list of vegetable crops with their key stats.

    Carrot 10 6 2 15 20
    Potato 15 8 2 25 35
    Onion 20 10 2 35 50
    Tomato 25 12 3 30 65
    Corn 30 14 2 50 70
    Pepper 35 16 2 60 85
    Rice 40 18 2 75 110
    Wheat 20 8 3 20 40

    Notes on Vegetables: - Carrots and Potatoes are ideal for early-game gold due to their short grow times. - Tomatoes and Corn offer the best profit per hour when planted in bulk. - Wheat is unique because it yields 3 units per harvest, making it excellent for cooking recipes.

    Fruit Crops

    Fruits take longer to grow but produce higher-value items. Many fruits are also used in advanced cooking recipes or as gifts for villagers. Fruit crops often require trellises or specific garden layouts.

    Crop Name Seed Cost (Gold) Grow Time (Hours) Harvest Yield Sell Price per Unit Profit per Harvest
    Blueberry 50 24 2 100 150
    Apple 60 30 2 120 180
    Peach 70 36 2 150 230
    Cherry 80 40 2 180 280
    Grape 90 48 3 130 300

    Notes on Fruits: - Grapes are the most profitable fruit per harvest, but they require the longest grow time. - Blueberries are a great mid-game crop with a reasonable 24-hour grow time. - Fruits are often used in juice-making or jam recipes, which can double their value.

    Special Crops

    Special crops include rare seeds obtained from events, quests, or high-level gardening. These crops often have unique properties, such as being used for special recipes or having a chance to produce multiple harvests.

    Crop Name Seed Cost (Gold) Grow Time (Hours) Harvest Yield Sell Price per Unit Profit per Harvest
    Starfruit 150 72 1 500 350
    Mushroom 100 48 2 200 300
    Spice Sprout 80 36 2 160 240
    Cotton 40 20 3 50 110

    Notes on Special Crops: - Starfruit is the highest-value single crop but has a long grow time and high seed cost. It is best used for gifting or cooking. - Mushrooms are a reliable source of income for mid-to-late game players. - Cotton is essential for crafting fabric and furniture, making it valuable beyond just selling.

    Profit Analysis and Best Crops by Strategy

    Best Crops for Early Game (First 20 Hours)

    If you are just starting out, focus on crops with low seed costs and fast grow times. Carrots and Potatoes are your best friends. Plant as many as your energy allows, and reinvest your profits into more seeds.

    • Carrot: 6-hour grow time, 20 gold profit per harvest. You can plant and harvest 4 times in a single day.
    • Potato: 8-hour grow time, 35 gold profit per harvest. Slightly better profit per hour than carrots.

    Best Crops for Mid Game (20-50 Hours)

    Once you have a steady gold flow, switch to higher-profit crops like Tomatoes, Corn, and Blueberries. These crops offer better returns per garden plot.

    • Tomato: 12-hour grow time, 65 gold profit per harvest. Great for cooking and selling.
    • Blueberry: 24-hour grow time, 150 gold profit per harvest. Perfect for daily harvest cycles.

    Best Crops for Late Game (50+ Hours)

    Late-game players should focus on Grapes, Starfruit, and Mushrooms. These crops require patience but yield the highest profits and are essential for end-game recipes.

    • Grape: 48-hour grow time, 300 gold profit per harvest. Best profit per plot.
    • Starfruit: 72-hour grow time, 350 gold profit per harvest. Ideal for special dishes.

    Gardening Mechanics and Tips

    Watering and Fertilizer

    All crops require regular watering. You can use a watering can or build sprinklers to automate the process. Fertilizer can be applied to reduce grow time or increase yield. Quality fertilizer reduces grow time by 20%, while Rich Fertilizer increases harvest yield by 1 unit.

    Crop Rotation and Soil Quality

    Soil quality degrades over time. Planting the same crop repeatedly reduces yield. Rotate your crops to maintain soil health. Using fertilizer also helps replenish soil nutrients.

    Multi-Harvest Crops

    Some crops, like Tomatoes and Grapes, can be harvested multiple times. After the initial harvest, the plant will regrow in half the original grow time. This makes them extremely efficient for long-term farming.

    Quick Tips

    • Start small: Focus on Carrots and Potatoes until you have at least 500 gold.
    • Automate early: Build sprinklers as soon as possible to save time and energy.
    • Use fertilizer: Quality Fertilizer on high-value crops like Grapes can significantly boost your daily profit.
    • Sell raw or cook: Cooking ingredients into meals often doubles or triples their value. Check recipes before selling. | - Gift wisely: Some villagers love specific crops. Gifting a Starfruit to a villager can increase friendship faster than any other gift. | - Plan your layout: Leave space for trellises if you plan to grow fruits like Grapes. A well-organized garden saves time and maximizes space. | - Check the market: Crop prices fluctuate daily. Sell when prices are high for maximum profit.

    9 (3×3) 5 Carrot + 4 Wheat 130 ~180 Fast turnaround, cooking staples
    12 (3×4) 6 Carrot + 3 Wheat + 3 Potato 165 ~260 Balanced profit and cooking use

    Strategy: Plant in the evening before logging off. Carrots (6h) are ready by morning, Wheat (8h) by midday. Harvest, sell, reinvest.

    Mid Game Combo (Gardening Lv 5-8)

    Plot Slots Crop Mix Total Cost Est. Daily Profit Why
    20 (4×5) 8 Tomato + 6 Corn + 6 Cabbage 582 ~500 Multi-harvest + high profit
    20 (4×5) 10 Cotton + 5 Bok Choy + 5 Rice 660 ~550 Cotton for crafting + food

    Strategy: Tomatoes and Corn give you multi-harvest — plant once, harvest repeatedly. Cabbage gives the best single-harvest profit. Cotton is essential for furniture crafting.

    20 (4×5) 8 Grape + 6 Starfruit + 6 Spice Sprout 1,720 ~1,600 End-game max efficiency

    Strategy: Ginger + Mushroom + Blueberry is your daily gold machine. Grape + Starfruit is for long-term investors (72h cycles). Use Quality Fertilizer on Ginger for star-quality bonuses.

    The "Set It and Forget It" Combo

    For players who can only log in once per day:

    Crop Why
    Blueberry (24h) Harvest once daily, multi-harvest
    Cabbage (48h) Harvest every other day, best profit/plot
    Tomato (12h) Can stretch to 24h without losing crop
    Rice (18h) Flexible timing, decent profit

    Plant a 50/50 mix of Blueberries and Cabbages for ~800 Gold/day with minimal effort.

    Star Quality Optimization

    Maximizing star-quality crops significantly boosts income:

    1. Quality Up Fertilizer on high-base-price crops (Ginger, Cabbage, Mushroom)
    2. Gardening skill levels passively increase star quality chance
    3. Special soil plots — home plot layouts with natural bonuses
    4. Sell star-quality crops individually — don't cook them unless a recipe specifically needs star ingredients

    Estimated income uplift: A full plot of Ginger with Quality Fertilizer yields ~3,000+ Gold/week vs. ~1,500 without.
