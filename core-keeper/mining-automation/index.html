    ⛏️ Core Keeper — Mining & Automation Guide

    Data sourced from Core Keeper 1.0 full release, Official Wiki, and player testing. Last updated July 2026.

    Quick Navigation: - How Mining Works - Pickaxe Tiers & Ore Progression - Drills & Automated Mining - Conveyor Belts & Sorting - Ancient Technology Table - Anvil System & Gear Upgrades - Mining Food Buffs & Gear Loadouts - Full Automation Blueprint - Pro Tips & Efficiency Guide

    ⛏️ How Mining Works

    Mining in Core Keeper is everything. Unlike Stardew Valley where farming is primary, Core Keeper's progression is gated entirely by what ore you can mine and what pickaxe you carry.

    Core Mining Mechanics

    Mechanic Detail
    Pickaxe Tier Determines which ores you can break
    Mining Speed Base speed × Pickaxe tier multiplier × Food/gear buffs
    Ore Spawning Ores are embedded in biome walls — follow the coloured veins
    Wall Types Dirt (all tiers break), Stone (copper+), Hard Stone (tin+), Bedrock (galaxite+)
    Swing Type Pickaxes have a wide arc — you can hit multiple adjacent blocks
    Range Bonus Some pickaxes have extended reach for mining tunnels

    💡 Hotkey Tip: Press R to switch between pickaxe and weapon quickly. Keep your pickaxe on the hotbar and your weapon on an adjacent slot for easy mining-to-combat transitions.

    Mining Speed Formula

    Effective Mining Speed = Base Pickaxe Speed × (1 + Mining Damage Bonuses)

    Each pickaxe has a base Mining Damage stat, and every point of damage roughly translates to ~0.5 seconds saved per block. Small bonuses compound significantly over a long mining session.

    🪓 Pickaxe Tiers & Ore Progression

    The Ore Tier Ladder

    Core Keeper has a strict tech ladder: you must craft each tier's pickaxe to access the next tier's ore. Skipping tiers is not possible.

    Copper → Tin → Iron → Gold → Scarlett → Galaxite

    🥇 Tier 1: Copper (Starting Area)

    Attribute Value
    Where to find Dirt Biome — brown/copper-coloured veins in stone walls
    Pickaxe needed Wooden Pickaxe (starter)
    Pickaxe materials 5 Wood + 3 Fibre
    Smelts into 1 Copper Bar per ore (3 seconds in Furnace)
    Usage Copper tools, Copper Furniture, Furnace, Workbench upgrades
    First priority Mine 20 Copper → Smelt bars → Build Furnace → Copper Pickaxe

    Early mining strategy: Break every copper vein you see. You need ~50 Copper Bars just for basic infrastructure. Don't sell copper ore — it's always more valuable as tools.

    🥈 Tier 2: Tin (Dirt Biome, Deeper)

    Attribute Value
    Where to find Dirt Biome — grey/white veins, often near water or below copper
    Pickaxe needed Copper Pickaxe (5 Copper Bars)
    Pickaxe materials 5 Copper Bars + 3 Wood
    Smelts into 1 Tin Bar per ore (3 seconds)
    Usage Tin tools, Tin Armour, Tin Sprinkler 🔑 (first automation!)
    First priority Mine 15 Tin → Craft Tin Pickaxe → Switch to Iron hunting

    🔑 Key milestone: Tin is where you unlock the Sprinkler (Gardening level 8) and the Fishing Rod. Once you have Tin gear, you're ready to fight Glurch and Ghorm.

    🥉 Tier 3: Iron (Clay Biome)

    Attribute Value
    Where to find Clay Biome — greenish-brown veins in clay walls
    Pickaxe needed Tin Pickaxe
    Pickaxe materials 8 Tin Bars + 3 Wood
    Smelts into 1 Iron Bar per ore (4 seconds)
    Usage Iron tools, Iron Armour, Iron Anvil 🛠️, Conveyor Belts 🔑
    First priority Mine 30 Iron → Craft Iron Anvil → Upgrade gear to Iron + Craft Conveyors

    ⚠️ Important: The Clay Biome is very large. Iron veins appear as green-brown deposits on the edges of clay walls. Look for colour transitions. If you've been mining clay for 5 minutes without finding iron, move in a different direction.

    🎯 Clay Biome navigation tip: The Clay Biome connects to the Dirt Biome through narrow passageways. Follow the brown clay ground texture. Iron veins are easiest to find along the border between Clay and Dirt areas.

    🏅 Tier 4: Gold (Forgotten Ruins)

    Attribute Value
    Where to find Forgotten Ruins — bright yellow veins 💛
    Pickaxe needed Iron Pickaxe
    Pickaxe materials 12 Iron Bars + 3 Wood
    Smelts into 1 Gold Bar per ore (5 seconds)
    Usage Gold tools, Gold Sprinkler (5×5 range!), Gold Anvil, Scarlet hunting gear
    First priority Mine 20 Gold → Craft Gold Pickaxe + Gold Sprinkler

    💡 Gold Pro Tip: Gold is actually not a major power jump for combat tools — the real prize at this stage is the Gold Sprinkler and the gold-tier upgrades that unlock access to the Scarlett biome. Don't linger too long in Forgotten Ruins; push toward the Mold biome once you have a Gold Pickaxe.

    🟣 Tier 5: Scarlett (Mold Biome)

    Attribute Value
    Where to find Mold Biome — deep purple/red veins 💜
    Pickaxe needed Gold Pickaxe
    Pickaxe materials 15 Gold Bars + 5 Fibre
    Smelts into 1 Scarlett Bar per ore (6 seconds)
    Usage Scarlett tools, Scarlett Armour, Scarlett Sprinkler (7×7!), Drills 🔥
    First priority Mine 40 Scarlett → Craft Scarlett Pickaxe → Build your first Drill

    🔥 Key Milestone: Scarlett is where automation truly begins. You unlock the Basic Drill, Advanced Drill, and the Scarlett-tier conveyor belt upgrades. This is the point where you can build a self-sustaining ore farming setup.

    How to reach the Mold Biome: From the Forgotten Ruins, follow purple-spore walls downward. The Mold Biome is characterised by: - Purple/magenta ground textures - Giant mushrooms everywhere - Spores in the air (harmless) - Glowing purple flora

    ⚪ Tier 6: Galaxite (Forlorn Metropolis)

    Attribute Value
    Where to find Forlorn Metropolis — glowing blue-white veins ✨
    Pickaxe needed Scarlett Pickaxe
    Pickaxe materials 20 Scarlett Bars + 5 Silk
    Smelts into 1 Galaxite Bar per ore (8 seconds)
    Usage Galaxite tools, Galaxite Armour, Galaxite+ upgrades, endgame gear
    First priority Mine 50 Galaxite → Craft Full Galaxite Gear → Upgrade at Passage

    💎 Galaxite is the endgame. With Galaxite tools and the Galaxite+ upgrade (from the Passage's Ancient Forge), you can mine any block in the game, including the unbreakable-looking black bedrock.

    Pickaxe Comparison Table

    Pickaxe Mining Damage Swing Speed Range Required For
    🪵 Wooden 3 1.0s 1 tile Breaking stone
    🥉 Copper 6 0.9s 1 tile Tin ore
    🥉 Tin 9 0.8s 1 tile Iron ore
    🔩 Iron 13 0.8s 2 tiles Gold ore, clay walls
    🥇 Gold 17 0.7s 2 tiles Scarlett ore
    🔮 Scarlett 22 0.6s 2 tiles Galaxite ore, bedrock
    ✨ Galaxite 28 0.5s 3 tiles Everything (endgame)
    🌟 Galaxite+ 35 0.4s 3 tiles Max mining speed

    💡 Extended range is a game-changer. The Iron Pickaxe's 2-tile range means you can mine through 1-block walls and see what's behind — invaluable for finding hidden ore pockets. Galaxite's 3-tile range lets you strip-mine entire rooms in seconds.

    🔩 Drills & Automated Mining

    Drills are the game-changing automation tool in Core Keeper. Once you have Scarlett-tier tech, drills let you mine ore automatically while you explore, fish, or fight bosses.

    Drill Tiers

    Drill Type Materials Range Mining Speed Ore per Hour (approx)
    🔧 Basic Drill 8 Scarlett Bars + 5 Gold Bars + 3 Mech Parts 3×3 (1 tile out) Slow ~60 ore
    ⚙️ Advanced Drill 12 Scarlett Bars + 8 Galaxite Bars + 5 Mech Parts 5×5 (2 tile out) Medium ~120 ore
    🏭 Massive Drill 20 Galaxite Bars + 10 Mech Parts + 5 Electronic Circuits 7×7 (3 tile out) Fast ~250 ore

    How Drills Work

    1. Place on any ore vein — the drill faces toward the wall
    2. Power it — connect to a power source (Electrical Generator or Solar Panel)
    3. Collect output — ore drops on the ground; use Conveyor Belts to move it to chests
    4. Duration — drills operate continuously as long as they have power + ore to mine
    5. Durability — drills have durability and need repair (use the Repair Station once worn)

    💡 Power requirements: Basic Drill = 1 power unit, Advanced = 2, Massive = 4. A single Electrical Generator (crafted from 10 Scarlett Bars + 5 Gold Bars) produces 5 power — enough for one Massive Drill + accessories.

    Drill Arm Upgrade

    In the Forlorn Metropolis, you can find the Drill Arm blueprint — this transforms any drill into a mobile mining tool that extends from your base.

    Drill Arm Materials Effect
    🔧 Basic Drill Arm 10 Mech Parts + 5 Iron Bars 3-tile reach, slow speed
    ⚙️ Advanced Drill Arm 15 Mech Parts + 8 Scarlett Bars 5-tile reach, medium speed
    🏭 Massive Drill Arm 20 Mech Parts + 10 Galaxite Bars 7-tile reach, fast speed

    ⚠️ Important: Drill Arms attach to the front of a drill and mine blocks beyond the drill's normal range. This lets you create long mining tunnels that the drill clears automatically. Perfect for strip-mining a whole biome.

    Best Drill Setup: The 3-Drill Array

    The most efficient setup for automated ore farming:

    Wall
├── Drill A (left vein) → Conveyor → Chest
├── Drill B (centre vein) → Conveyor → Chest  
└── Drill C (right vein) → Conveyor → Chest
    ↑
Power Generator (centre, feeds all 3)

    Why 3 drills work best: Most large ore deposits are 3–4 blocks wide. Three Basic Drills cover the entire deposit. As veins deplete, the middle drill keeps going while you reposition the side drills.

    📦 Conveyor Belts & Sorting

    Conveyor Belts unlock at the Iron Anvil and are your primary logistics tool. They transport items from drills/farms straight into chests.

    Conveyor Belt Types

    Belt Type Materials Speed Turns
    🟤 Basic Belt 3 Iron Bars + 2 Wood Slow (1 tile/sec) Manual (place corners)
    ⚪ Iron Belt 5 Iron Bars + 3 Mech Parts Medium (2 tiles/sec) Snap-turns (90°)
    🟣 Scarlett Belt 8 Scarlett Bars + 5 Mech Parts Fast (3 tiles/sec) Auto-turns, slopes
    ✨ Galaxite Belt 10 Galaxite Bars + 5 Electronic Circuits Very Fast (4 tiles/sec) Auto-turns, slopes, splitters 🎯

    Sorting System Blueprint

    A proper sorting system uses Item Sorters (unlocked at the Ancient Technology Table) to separate different ores into different chests:

    Drill Output → Conveyor → Item Sorter (configured for Copper)
                              ↓ (not copper)
                         Item Sorter (configured for Tin)
                              ↓ (not tin)
                         Item Sorter (configured for Iron)
                              ↓ (not iron)
                    Catch-all Chest (gems, rare drops)

    How to Build Item Sorters

    1. Unlock Packaging & Logistics at the Ancient Technology Table (costs 3 Ancient Gemstones)
    2. Craft an Item Sorter (5 Gold Bars + 3 Mech Parts + 2 Fibre)
    3. Place it on a conveyor line
    4. Interact with it → select which item to filter
    5. Route the sorted output to a chest via conveyor

    💡 Pro Tip: Build a central sorting hub at your base and run all drill outputs to it via one long conveyor line. This is far more efficient than running individual lines to separate chests.

    🏛️ Ancient Technology Table

    The Ancient Technology Table is your tech tree progression system. It uses Ancient Gemstones (boss drops + loot from ruined chests) to unlock permanent upgrades.

    Key Tech Upgrades (Priority Order)

    Upgrade Gemstone Cost Effect Priority
    🔧 Tool Upgrade I 3 +10% Mining Speed 🥇 First
    ⚙️ Tool Upgrade II 8 +20% Mining Speed 🥇 After Scarlett
    📦 Packaging & Logistics 3 Unlock Item Sorters 🥇 Early automation
    ⚡ Electrical Systems 5 Unlock Generators + Drills 🥇 Mid-game
    🏭 Advanced Automation 10 Unlock Advanced Drills 🥈 After Scarlett
    🔋 Power Efficiency 8 -20% drill power consumption 🥈 Optimization
    🛡️ Armour Reinforcements 5 +15% Defence 🥉 Before bosses
    🌟 Master Smith 15 +25% gear craft quality 🥉 Endgame
    💎 Gemstone Mastery 20 +50% Ancient Gemstone drops 🥈 Once available

    💡 Gemstone farming priority: Ancient Gemstones drop from every boss and appear in Forgotten Ruins chests. You need ~60 total for all upgrades. Never sell them — they're more valuable than gold.

    🛠️ Anvil System & Gear Upgrades

    Anvils are how you upgrade your equipment from one tier to the next. Each anvil unlocks progressively better gear.

    Anvil Tiers

    Anvil Materials Unlocks Upgradeable From
    🥉 Copper Anvil 8 Copper Bars Copper → Tin Starting tools
    🔩 Iron Anvil 12 Iron Bars Tin → Iron Copper Anvil
    🥇 Gold Anvil 15 Gold Bars + 5 Iron Bars Iron → Gold Iron Anvil
    🔮 Scarlett Anvil 20 Scarlett Bars + 8 Gold Bars Gold → Scarlett Gold Anvil
    ✨ Galaxite Anvil 25 Galaxite Bars + 10 Scarlett Bars Scarlett → Galaxite Scarlett Anvil

    Upgrade Costs Per Slot

    Each piece of gear (pickaxe, weapon, helmet, chestplate, boots, shield) costs:

    Upgrade Ore Bars Per Piece Total for Full Set
    Copper → Tin 5 Tin Bars 30 Bars
    Tin → Iron 8 Iron Bars 48 Bars
    Iron → Gold 12 Gold Bars 72 Bars
    Gold → Scarlett 18 Scarlett Bars 108 Bars
    Scarlett → Galaxite 25 Galaxite Bars 150 Bars

    ⚠️ Math check: A full Galaxite gear set costs 150 Galaxite Bars = 150 ore. With a Massive Drill, that's about 36 minutes of passive mining. Without drills, expect 2–3 hours of dedicated mining.

    🏭 Full Automation Blueprint

    Self-Sustaining Mining Outpost

    Here's a blueprint for a fully automated outpost that mines, sorts, and stores ore without any player intervention:

    ⚡ POWER SECTION
[Solar Panels × 4 or Electrical Generator × 2] → Power Relay → Drills

⛏️ MINING SECTION  
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│  Massive Drill → Conveyor Belt   │
│  Massive Drill → Conveyor Belt   │
│  Massive Drill → Conveyor Belt   │

         ↓
📦 SORTING SECTION

│  Item Sorter: Galaxite → Chest  │
│  Item Sorter: Scarlett → Chest  │
│  Item Sorter: Gold → Chest      │
│  Catch-All → Overflow Chest     │
└─────────────────────────────────┘

    Required Materials for Full Automation

    Item Qty Materials Total
    Massive Drill 3 60 Galaxite Bars + 30 Mech Parts
    Conveyor Belt (Galaxite) 40 400 Galaxite Bars + 200 Circuits
    Item Sorter 4 20 Gold Bars + 12 Mech Parts
    Generator 2 20 Scarlett Bars + 10 Gold Bars
    Large Chest 6 120 Wood

    Step-by-Step Setup

    1. Find a multi-vein ore deposit — ideally 3+ adjacent Galaxite veins
    2. Clear a 5×5 room in front of the deposit for your drills
    3. Place 3 Massive Drills facing the ore wall
    4. Run Conveyor Belts from each drill to a central line
    5. Install Item Sorters above each target chest
    6. Place 2 Generators with fuel (coal or wood) — or Solar Panels on the surface
    7. Test the system — drop a few ore by hand to verify sorting
    8. Done — collect full chests of sorted ore every hour

    💡 Pro Tips & Efficiency Guide

    1. The "Staircase Mining" Method

    Don't mine horizontally — mine at a 45° downward staircase. This lets you cover more ground and reveals ore veins 2× faster because you're cross-sectioning multiple biome layers simultaneously.

    2. Wall-Marking System

    Use different-coloured torches or floor tiles to mark ore locations: - 🟠 Copper: Orange torch - ⚪ Tin: White torch - 🟢 Iron: Green torch - 🟡 Gold: Yellow torch - 🟣 Scarlett: Purple torch - 🔵 Galaxite: Blue torch

    3. Don't Leave Half-Mined Veins

    Ore veins in Core Keeper have a hidden adjacency bonus — mining connected ore blocks is slightly faster. Leave a vein partially mined and the exposed blocks actually slow your pickaxe. Always mine from the outside in.

    4. Use Terrain to Your Advantage

    • Water slows you down — bridge across underground lakes instead of swimming
    • Lava deals damage — use Scarlett Boots for lava immunity
    • Darkness spawns mobs — place torches as you mine, not after

    5. The 10-Minute Rule

    Spend no more than 10 minutes mining in one spot before moving to a new location. Ore veins deplete visibly, and the game respawns new ore only when you're far enough away. Moving resets the spawn timer.

    6. Repair Station Location

    Always place a Repair Station next to your drill array. Drills wear down over time, and walking back to base to repair them wastes time you could spend exploring.

    7. Prioritise Speed Over Storage

    A common mistake is building huge chests before upgrading mining speed. The order should be: 1. 🥇 Pickaxe upgrade (+speed) 2. 🥇 Mining food buffs (+speed) 3. 🥈 Better boots (+move speed between veins) 4. 🥉 Better ring (+mining damage) 5. 🏆 Chests (storage only matters once you're fast enough to fill them)
