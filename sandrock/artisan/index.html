    🔧 My Time at Sandrock Artisan Guide — Processing, Refining, and Crafting

    Sandrock is a workshop town. Unlike farming sims where crops drive the economy, Sandrock's money comes from processing raw materials into refined goods. Ore goes into the furnace, scrap goes into the recycler, and sand goes into the grinder. Understanding the processing chains — and which ones make the most Gols per hour — is the key to building a profitable workshop.

    Tip: Never sell raw ore or scrap. Processed bars and refined materials sell for 2-5× the raw value. Your workshop machines are literally printing money — keep them running 24/7.

    🏭 Processing Machines Overview

    You start with a basic Furnace and Recycler. More machines unlock as you level your workshop and buy blueprints from the Commerce Guild.

    Machine Unlock Cost Input Output Speed
    🔥 Furnace Starting Free Ore → Bars Copper / Iron / Steel / Manganese / Titanium 1 bar per 2h (base)
    ♻️ Recycler Starting Free Scrap → Materials Wood / Fiber / Rubber / Parts 1 item per 1h
    ⚙️ Grinder Workshop Lv.2 1,200g Sand → Glass / Quartz Glass / Quartz Powder 1 per 40m
    🧵 Loom Workshop Lv.3 2,500g Fiber → Fabric / Rope Cloth, Rope 1 per 2h
    🔩 Compressor Workshop Lv.4 3,500g Bars → Alloy Plates Steel Plate, Manganese Plate 1 per 3h
    ⚗️ Refiner Workshop Lv.5 5,000g Processed → Premium Refined Quartz, Pure Silicon 1 per 4h
    🪵 Drying Rack Workshop Lv.2 800g Food → Preserved Dried Food, Jerky 1 per 6h

    Workshop Layout Rule: Place machines with 1 tile of space between them. You'll need room to walk around and collect outputs. A 3×3 grid of mixed machines fits nicely in a medium workshop.

    Tip: Build 2-3 furnaces before anything else. Ore processing is your bottleneck in early game. One furnace can't keep up with your mining output once you're past the copper age.

    🔥 Furnace — Ore to Bars

    The furnace is your most important machine. Every bar you smelt is worth 3-5× the raw ore.

    Ore Type Raw Value (1 piece) Smelted Bar Bar Sell Price Profit Multiplier
    🥉 Copper Ore 5 Gols Copper Bar 60 Gols 12×
    🥈 Iron Ore 8 Gols Iron Bar 100 Gols 12.5×
    ⚙️ Scrap Iron 3 Gols Iron Bar (mixed) 100 Gols 8×
    🔩 Steel Scrap 12 Gols Steel Bar 160 Gols 3.3×
    ⚔️ Manganese Ore 20 Gols Manganese Bar 280 Gols 3.5×
    💎 Titanium Ore 35 Gols Titanium Bar 500 Gols 3.6×

    Processing Math: 5 Copper Ore (25 Gols) → 1 Copper Bar (60 Gols) = +35 Gols profit per bar. In a 2-hour cycle, that's 17.5 Gols/hour per furnace. Seems small, but 3 furnaces running 12 hours = 630 Gols from copper alone.

    Furnace Upgrade Priority

    Upgrade Cost Effect Priority
    🔥 Fuel Efficiency 1,000g -20% fuel per bar ⭐ Medium
    ⏱️ Speed Upgrade 2,000g -30% smelt time ⭐⭐⭐ High
    🔄 Batch Processing 3,000g Smelt 2 bars at once ⭐⭐⭐ Highest
    🧪 Quality Boost 2,500g +1 quality tier on bars ⭐⭐ Medium

    Tip: Batch Processing is the single best furnace upgrade. It effectively doubles your furnace capacity. Get it before Speed Upgrade — double output beats 30% faster for the same cost.

    ⚙️ Grinder — Sand and Glass

    The grinder turns desert sand into valuable glass and quartz products. Sand is literally free (it's everywhere) and glass sells for 80 Gols per piece.

    Input Output Time Sell Price Profit/Hour Notes
    Sand ×3 Glass ×1 40m 80 Gols 120 Gols/h ✅ Best early profit
    Sand ×5 Clear Glass ×1 1h 120 Gols 120 Gols/h Same profit, better for crafting
    Quartz ×2 Quartz Powder ×1 30m 50 Gols 100 Gols/h Used in advanced recipes
    Sand ×2 + Dye Colored Glass ×1 1.5h 150 Gols 100 Gols/h Festivals only

    The Sand Economy: One inventory load of sand (20 pieces) can be gathered in about 2 minutes on the eastern dunes. That's 6-7 glass pieces = 480-560 Gols from 2 minutes of work. Sand grinding is the single best low-effort moneymaker in early Sandrock.

    Tip: Set up 2 Grinders as soon as you can afford them. One dedicated to glass, one for quartz powder. Glass funds your early workshop expansion, and quartz powder is needed for later upgrades anyway.

    ♻️ Recycler — Scrap to Materials

    The recycler turns the junk you find in ruins, alleys, and the scrapyard into usable materials. It's the best way to get wood in the desert (trees are scarce) and nets you rare parts.

    Scrap Type Input Output Options Best Output Time
    🪵 Wood Scrap 1 Wood ×2-4, Fiber ×1 Wood ×4 1h
    🔩 Metal Scrap 1 Iron Ore ×2-3, Copper Ore ×2 Iron Ore ×3 1h
    🔧 Machinery Scrap 1 Steel Bar ×1, Springs ×1, Parts ×2 Parts ×2 1.5h
    🧪 Chemical Scrap 1 Acid ×1, Dye ×1, Oil ×1 Oil ×1 1h
    ♻️ Mixed Scrap 1 Random (low tier) Varies 1h

    Scrap Hunting Routes

    Location Best Scrap Type Respawn Time Per Run Value
    🏚️ Abandoned Ruins (E-1) Machinery Scrap Daily ~400 Gols
    🚂 Train Yard Metal + Wood Scrap 2 days ~300 Gols
    🗑️ Town Alleyways Mixed Scrap 3 days ~150 Gols
    🏜️ Desert Oasis Chemical Scrap Weekly ~200 Gols

    Tip: Always pick up Machinery Scrap — it's the rarest and gives the most valuable outputs. Springs and Parts are used in almost every advanced blueprint. Wood Scrap is common but vital since trees don't grow fast in the desert.

    🧵 Loom — Fabric Production

    The loom turns plant fiber into cloth, rope, and fabric. It's crucial for clothing, furniture, and high-end workshop upgrades.

    Recipe Fiber Needed Time Output Sell Price Use Case
    Cloth 10 2h 1 Cloth 150 Gols Furniture, clothing
    Rope 8 1.5h 2 Rope 80 Gols (×2=160) Machines, decorations
    Silk Fabric 15 3h 1 Silk 250 Gols Luxury furniture
    Canvas 12 2.5h 1 Canvas 200 Gols Workshop upgrades

    Fiber Sources: Harvest from desert bushes (renewable every 3 days), grow cotton on your farm, or buy from the Commerce Guild for 8 Gols each. Growing cotton is the most efficient — 1 cotton plant gives 5-8 fiber per harvest.

    Tip: Rope is more efficient than Cloth for pure profit — 16 fiber (8 fiber × 2 rope) = 160 Gols vs 10 fiber = 150 Gols for cloth. But cloth is needed for more recipes. Run both.

    💰 Profit Optimization — Best Processing Chains

    These are the most profitable processing chains ranked by Gols per hour.

    Early Game (Workshop Lv.1-3)

    Chain Steps Investment Profit/Day Best For
    🥇 Sand → Glass 1 step (Grinder) 1,200g 1,440 Gols ✅ Best early moneymaker
    🥈 Scrap → Parts 1 step (Recycler) Free 600-800 Gols Passive income
    🥉 Ore → Bar 1 step (Furnace) Free 400-600 Gols Core progression

    Mid Game (Workshop Lv.4-5)

    Chain Steps Profit/Day Notes
    🥇 Ore → Bar → Plate 2 steps (Furnace + Compressor) 2,400-3,200 Gols Alloy plates sell high
    🥈 Fiber → Cloth + Rope 2 steps (Cotton + Loom) 1,800 Gols Requires cotton planting
    🥉 Sand → Quartz → Refined 2 steps (Grinder + Refiner) 2,000 Gols Refined quartz sells for 300g

    Late Game (Workshop Lv.5+)

    Chain Steps Profit/Day Notes
    🥇 Titanium Ore → Titanium Bar → Alloy 2 steps 6,000+ Gols Best endgame chain
    🥈 Complex Scrap → Rare Parts → Assemblies 2-3 steps 4,000-5,000 Gols Parts are always in demand
    🥉 Mixed Chains (build to order) Variable 8,000-10,000 Gols Commission-focused

    ⚡ Best Processing Chains by Goal

    Goal Best Chain Why
    💰 Pure Profit Sand → Glass (early) / Titanium Alloy (late) Lowest effort, highest margin
    🛠️ Commission Prep Ore → Bar → Plate Most commissions need plates
    🏠 House Building Wood Scrap → Wood + Fiber → Rope House upgrades need tons of rope
    🎁 Gifting Fiber → Cloth → Tailored Clothing High friendship value
    ⚔️ Combat Prep Ore → Manganese/Steel Bar → Weapon Parts Weapon upgrades need specific bars

    Tip: Don't optimize purely for profit. Check the Commerce Guild board daily for commissions. Some "sub-optimal" chains earn more via commissions than selling on the open market. A commission asking for 5 Glass Panels pays 200% of market price.

    🪵 Drying Rack — Passive Food Income

    The drying rack preserves food for longer storage and slightly higher sell prices.

    Input Output Time Sell Price Notes
    Meat ×1 Jerky ×1 6h 120 Gols 3× base meat value
    Cactus ×3 Dried Cactus ×1 4h 80 Gols Decent filler
    Fish ×1 Dried Fish ×1 5h 100-180 Gols Depends on fish type
    Fruit ×5 Dried Fruit ×1 6h 150 Gols Not worth it

    Tip: Drying racks are low-priority early on. Build 1 after your furnaces and grinders are set up. Jerky is the most profitable — meat from hunting is abundant and sells for 40g raw but 120g dried.

    📊 Quick Reference — Machine Count

    Phase Furnaces Grinders Recyclers Looms Compressors Refiners
    🌱 Early (Lv.1-2) 2-3 1-2 1 0 0 0
    🌿 Mid (Lv.3-4) 3-4 2 2 1 1 1
    🌳 Late (Lv.5+) 4-6 3 2 2 2 2

    Tip: These counts assume you're doing full-day mining runs. If you commission-hunt more, add an extra Furnace. If you sand-grind more, add Grinders. Adjust based on your playstyle.
